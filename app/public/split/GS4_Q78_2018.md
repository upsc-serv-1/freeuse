## Question 78 (Year: 2018 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2018-gs4-q78-civilsdaily**

**2018 - “Falsehood takes the place of truth when it results in unblemished common good.” -Tirukkural** [Year: 2018] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

The above statement highlights the “**teleological”** approach to truth. It suggests that the moral value of an action is determined not just by the act itself, but by the **ultimate outcome** it produces for society.

### Justification of Falsehood

1. **Utilitarian ethics -** Maximising overall welfare. Eg- During COVID-19, some governments delayed announcing lockdowns to prevent mass panic.

2. **Principle of non-maleficence -** Preventing harm. Eg- Doctors not disclosing terminal diagnoses to prevent severe psychological shock.

3. **Doctrine of double effect -** Harm avoided outweighs moral cost. Eg- Undercover police deceive suspects to dismantle trafficking networks.

4. **To maintain social harmony.** Eg- administration declaring minor scuffle between two communities as a "personal dispute" to prevent a city-wide riot.

5. **Emergency ethics -** in wartime, truth is so precious that she should always be attended by a bodyguard of lies. - **Winston Churchill.**

6. **Public interest principle -** Eg- Withholding sensitive information during terror attacks to avoid aiding perpetrators.

7. **Virtue ethics -** Compassion and prudence over rigid honesty.

8. **Protection of life - Right to life supersedes right to information.** Eg- providing **"New Identities"** under **witness protection**

9. **Diplomatic De-escalation-** During war, **"back-channel" communications** involve **tactical denials** to give face-saving exit to both countries.

### Counter-argument

1. **Erodes trust** in governance.

2. **Slippery slope** - “Noble lies” justify authoritarianism.

3. “hiding failures.” Eg- falsifying COVID deaths

4. **Violates autonomy** and **right to truth**.

5. **Accountability loss** - Lies prevent public scrutiny.

6. **Long-term harm** - Eg- initial denials of **Chernobyl’s** severity

7. **Moral corrosion** - Ends justify means mentality spreads injustice.

The Tirukkural does not provide a "license to lie" but rather a **"test of virtue."** Lie is justified only when the good produced is "unblemished" (meaning it harms no one else) and "common" (benefiting all).
