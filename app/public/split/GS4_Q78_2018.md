## Question 78 (Year: 2018 | Paper: GS IV)

**2018 - “Falsehood takes the place of truth when it results in unblemished common good.” -Tirukkural [Year: 2018] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

The above statement highlights the “**teleological” approach to truth. It suggests that the moral value of an action is determined not just by the act itself, but by the **ultimate outcome it produces for society.
### **Justification of Falsehood
1. Utilitarian ethics - Maximising overall welfare. Eg- During COVID-19, some governments delayed announcing lockdowns to prevent mass panic.
1. Principle of non-maleficence - Preventing harm. Eg- Doctors not disclosing terminal diagnoses to prevent severe psychological shock.
1. Doctrine of double effect - Harm avoided outweighs moral cost. Eg- Undercover police deceive suspects to dismantle trafficking networks.
1. To maintain social harmony. Eg- administration declaring minor scuffle between two communities as a "personal dispute" to prevent a city-wide riot.
1. Emergency ethics - in wartime, truth is so precious that she should always be attended by a bodyguard of lies. - **Winston Churchill.
1. Public interest principle - Eg- Withholding sensitive information during terror attacks to avoid aiding perpetrators.
### Virtue ethics - Compassion and prudence over rigid honesty.
1. Protection of life - Right to life supersedes right to information. Eg- providing **"New Identities" under **witness protection
1. Diplomatic De-escalation- During war, **"back-channel" communications involve **tactical denials to give face-saving exit to both countries.
### **Counter-argument
1. Erodes trust in governance.
### Slippery slope - “Noble lies” justify authoritarianism.
1. “hiding failures.” Eg- falsifying COVID deaths
1. Violates autonomy and **right to truth.
### Accountability loss - Lies prevent public scrutiny.
1. Long-term harm - Eg- initial denials of **Chernobyl’s severity
1. Moral corrosion - Ends justify means mentality spreads injustice.
The Tirukkural does not provide a "license to lie" but rather a **"test of virtue." Lie is justified only when the good produced is "unblemished" (meaning it harms no one else) and "common" (benefiting all).
