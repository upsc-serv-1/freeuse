## Question 73 (Year: 2022 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2022-gs4-q73-civilsdaily**

**2022 - In the contemporary world, the corporate sector’s contribution in generating wealth and employment is increasing. In doing so, they are bringing in unprecedented onslaught on the climate, environmental sustainability and living conditions of human beings. In this background, are Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) efficient and sufficient enough to fulfill the social roles and responsibilities needed in the corporate work mandated? Critically examine.** [Year: 2022] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

**CSR **refers to the responsibility of companies to operate ethically, contribute to sustainable development, and minimise negative social and environmental impacts while pursuing profits.

“The business of business is not just business.”

**The Paradox- Wealth Generation vs. Environmental Onslaught**

| **Economic Contribution** | **Environmental / Social Cost** |

| --- | --- |

| **Coal mining in Chhattisgarh **generates jobs | Forest loss and displacement of tribal communities |

| **Thermal power plants **fuel industrial growth | Air pollution and health hazards (Delhi NCR) |

| **Fast fashion industry **boosts exports | water pollution (Tiruppur dyeing units) |

| **Construction sector **drives urban growth | **River sand mining **and groundwater depletion |

| **IT data centres **enable digital economy | High **energy use **and **carbon footprint** |

| **E-commerce Expansion **- gig employment | Microplastic pollution |

**CSR fulfilling social role and responsibilities**

1.** Mandatory Compliance in **India (2% of profits) has channeled nearly **₹25,000 crore annually **into social sectors.

2.** Disaster Response- **Eg- Reliance funded plants to produce medical oxygen during COVID.

3.** Innovation in Education- **Eg- "Hole-in-the-wall" project by Sony and NIIT for computer literacy to slum children.

4.** Project Nanhi Kali **(Mahindra Group) - Providing 10 years of academic support and material kits to underprivileged girls to reduce dropout rates.

5.** Healthcare Infrastructure- **Eg- Vedanta’s "Nand Ghar" initiative for transforming Anganwadis into modern child welfare centers.

6.** Skill Development- **Eg- Mahindra & Mahindra’s "Hunar" program for training in specialized technical skills.

7.** Kaushalya **(Tata Motors) - Training unemployed youth using a "Learn and Earn" model.

8.** Sanitation & Water- **Eg- ITC’s "Mission Sunehra Kal" for promoting "Climate Smart" agriculture through watershed development

9.** Transition to Renewable Energy- **Eg- Adani Foundation’s projects for solar-powered rural electrification.

10.** Women Empowerment- **Eg- HUL’s "Project Shakti" for creating a network of micro-entrepreneurs ("Shakti Ammas") to sell hygiene products.

### Issues with CSR

1.** Greenwashing- **Using CSR for PR. Eg- Coca-Cola’s "World Without Waste" campaign while being world's top plastic polluter for years.

2.** Peripheral Activity- **CSR is often a "side project".

3.** Geographical Bias- **concentrated in industrialized states, ignoring backward regions like Bihar or the Northeast.

4.** The "Checklist" Approach- **focus on spending the money rather than the impact or outcome.

5.** Lack of Expertise, leading to poorly designed projects.

6. CSR (2%) is a **fraction **of the cost of the "Externalities" the company causes

7.** Crony CSR- **channeling funds into "in-house" foundations or politically connected NGOs.

8.** Difficulty in Measuring Impact- **no universal standard to quantify the "Social Return on Investment" (SROI).

### A Way Forward

1.** ESG Integration in **core investment and operational strategy.

2.** Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) **for the entire lifecycle of product. Eg- plastic waste management.

3.** Impact measurement **through third-party ethical audits.

4.** Circular Economy- **Shifting from "Take-Make-Dispose" model to "Reduce-Reuse-Recycle."

True sustainability requires **Systems Change, where profit and planet are seen as mutually inclusive.
