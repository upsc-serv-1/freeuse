## Question 20 (Year: 2021 | Paper: GS IV)

**2021 - “Refugees should not be turned back to the country where they would face persecution or human right violation.” Examine the statement with reference to ethical dimension being violated by the nation claiming to be democratic with open society. [Year: 2021] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

Violation of **principle of non-refoulement creates a **"Liberal Paradox," where the values used to define the state are discarded at its borders.
### **Refugee Rights
1. Right to Asylum (Article 14 of the UDHR).
1. Right to Life and Liberty
1. Right to Non-Discrimination
1. Freedom from Torture
1. Right to Due Process
1. Family unity
1. Access to basic services
### **Ethical Dimensions Violated by Refoulement
1. Violation of Kantian Categorical imperative- treats refugees as mere "means" to a political end border security.
1. Violation of **Justice as Fairness (John Rawls) by sending refugees to face **persecution or human right violation
1. Breach of Universal values like liberty, right to life. Eg- Alyan Kurdi death
1. Erosion of Human Dignity and an active denial of their **Intrinsic Worth. Eg- Detention camps for asylum seekers.
1. Loss of moral leadership and **global solidarity- Undermines credibility in human rights advocacy
1. Erosion of rule of law - Ignoring international refugee conventions. Eg- Violations of 1951 Refugee Convention.
1. Failure of the "Good Samaritan" Principle i.e. preventing great harm (death) with comparatively small cost.
1. Failure of humanitarian duty - Moral obligation to protect the vulnerable is ignored.
1. Discrimination - Selective hospitality based on race, religion, or politics. Eg- Preferential treatment of Ukrainian refugees over African refugees in Europe.
1. Epistemic Injustice- Refusing to believe or listen to the testimony of a persecuted person before deportation.
1. Many refugee crises (Syria, Afghanistan, Myanmar) are linked to historical colonial or military interventions by democratic powers. Refoulement is a rejection of **Restorative Justice.
1. The "Slippery Slope" of Rights- Eg- devaluing the rights of its own marginalized citizens in future
1. Failure of Communitarian Ethics as nations are seen as part of a global "International Society" with mutual duties.
1. Causes **long-term psychological and ethical decay in the host nation’s own moral fabric.
**Way Forward
1. Individualized Screening- Eg-l "Credible Fear" interview.
1. Judicial Oversight to ensure **Procedural Justice.
1. Principle of Proportionality. **in Enforcement
1. Burden Sharing. Eg- EU Migration Pact
1. Enforcing **"Responsibility to Protect" by UN
The ultimate best practice for a democratic society is to apply the **Rawlsian "Veil of Ignorance" to every policy.
