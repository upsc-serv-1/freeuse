## Question 75 (Year: 2019 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2019-gs4-q75-civilsdaily**

**2019 - Effective utilization of public funds is crucial to meet development goals. Critically examine the reasons for under-utilization and mis-utilization of public funds and their implications.** [Year: 2019] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

Resource stewardship is the hallmark of **good governance** and is essential for translating fiscal resources into social welfare. Its poor utilisation violates the **“trusteeship” principle (Locke)**

### Importance of Effective Utilization of Public Funds

**Achieving Social Justice-** Tribal sub plan funds reducing historical inequality.

**Ensure timely completion of infrastructure projects to** drive long-term GDP growth.

**Human Capital Formation** through improved public service delivery **Poverty Alleviation-** Eg- DBT provide a safety net for those below the poverty line.

**Fiscal Discipline-** Prevents unnecessary borrowing and reduces the fiscal deficit

**Builds Public Trust** in government - ensures better tax-compliance **Attracting Investment-** "Crowding-in" effect.

### Reasons for Under-Utilization

1. **Administrative Red Tapism-** Lengthy approval processes for tenders and contracts.

2. **Late fund release** lead to **“March Rush”**

3. **Capacity Constraints-** Local bodies lack technical staff to draft Detailed Project Reports (DPRs).

4. **One-size-fits-all guidelines** limits flexibility. Eg- CSS

5. **Land acquisition delays** stalls major infrastructure projects.

6. **Low accountability-** lack of real-time tracking of funds

7. **Fear of the "3 Cs"(CBI, CVC, CAG)** causes bureaucratic paralysis

### Reasons for Mis-Utilization

1. **Corruption** through **ghost beneficiaries.** Eg- over 10 lakh fake accounts in Ayushman Bharat (CAG)

2. **Political populism -** Diverting long-term developmental funds into short-term electoral "gifts".

3. **Poor accountability mechanisms.** Eg- lack of social audits

4. **Absence of Outcome Budgeting-** Focus on "outlays" rather than "impact" (lives changed).

5. **Institutionalized Rent-Seeking-** The presence of middlemen in every stage of fund dispersal.

**6. Politician-bureaucrat-corporate nexus (Vohra committee) -** leads to **culture of impunity**

**Implications**

1. Violates **utilitarian maxim** of greatest happiness of greatest number

2. Leads to **inequality -** 1% holding >40% national wealth (**Oxfam**)

3. **Poor quality of service** is delivered to citizens. Eg- rotten foodgrains in PDS

4. **Cost Overruns-** Delays lead to inflation-adjusted increases in project costs.

5. **Delayed Benefits-** The public is deprived of essential services

6. **Opportunity Cost-** Capital that could have been used elsewhere remains locked and unproductive.

7. **Economic Distortion-** Unproductive spending leads to inflation without asset creation.

8. **Erosion of Ethics-** Normalizes a culture of dishonesty within the civil service.

9. Leads to **"Taxpayer Cynicism"** - encourage tax evasion.

Public servants are **trustee** of public funds. It must be used with **responsibility and integrity.**
