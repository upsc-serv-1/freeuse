## Question 75 (Year: 2019 | Paper: GS IV)

**2019 - Effective utilization of public funds is crucial to meet development goals. Critically examine the reasons for under-utilization and mis-utilization of public funds and their implications. [Year: 2019] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

Resource stewardship is the hallmark of **good governance and is essential for translating fiscal resources into social welfare. Its poor utilisation violates the **“trusteeship” principle (Locke)
**Importance of Effective Utilization of Public Funds
**Achieving Social Justice- Tribal sub plan funds reducing historical inequality.
**Ensure timely completion of infrastructure projects to drive long-term GDP growth.
**Human Capital Formation through improved public service delivery **Poverty Alleviation- Eg- DBT provide a safety net for those below the poverty line.
**Fiscal Discipline- Prevents unnecessary borrowing and reduces the fiscal deficit
**Builds Public Trust in government - ensures better tax-compliance **Attracting Investment- "Crowding-in" effect.
### **Reasons for Under-Utilization
1. Administrative Red Tapism- Lengthy approval processes for tenders and contracts.
1. Late fund release lead to **“March Rush”
1. Capacity Constraints- Local bodies lack technical staff to draft Detailed Project Reports (DPRs).
1. One-size-fits-all guidelines limits flexibility. Eg- CSS
1. Land acquisition delays stalls major infrastructure projects.
1. Low accountability- lack of real-time tracking of funds
1. Fear of the "3 Cs"(CBI, CVC, CAG) causes bureaucratic paralysis
### **Reasons for Mis-Utilization
1. Corruption through **ghost beneficiaries. Eg- over 10 lakh fake accounts in Ayushman Bharat (CAG)
1. Political populism - Diverting long-term developmental funds into short-term electoral "gifts".
1. Poor accountability mechanisms. Eg- lack of social audits
1. Absence of Outcome Budgeting- Focus on "outlays" rather than "impact" (lives changed).
1. Institutionalized Rent-Seeking- The presence of middlemen in every stage of fund dispersal.
1. **6. Politician-bureaucrat-corporate nexus (Vohra committee) - leads to **culture of impunity
**Implications
1. Violates **utilitarian maxim of greatest happiness of greatest number
1. Leads to **inequality - 1% holding >40% national wealth (**Oxfam)
1. Poor quality of service is delivered to citizens. Eg- rotten foodgrains in PDS
1. Cost Overruns- Delays lead to inflation-adjusted increases in project costs.
1. Delayed Benefits- The public is deprived of essential services
1. Opportunity Cost- Capital that could have been used elsewhere remains locked and unproductive.
1. Economic Distortion- Unproductive spending leads to inflation without asset creation.
1. Erosion of Ethics- Normalizes a culture of dishonesty within the civil service.
### Leads to **"Taxpayer Cynicism" - encourage tax evasion.
Public servants are **trustee of public funds. It must be used with **responsibility and integrity.
