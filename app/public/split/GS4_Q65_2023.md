## Question 65 (Year: 2023 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2023-gs4-q65-civilsdaily**

**2023 - Is conscience a more reliable guide when compared to laws, rules and regulations in the context of ethical decision making ? Discuss.** [Year: 2023] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

**Conscience** is the "inner voice" or the internal moral sense of what is right or wrong in one's conduct. “There is a higher court than courts of justice and that is the court of conscience. It supersedes all other courts.” - **Mahatma Gandhi Conscience as a Reliable Guide**

1. Conscience addresses the **"spirit" of justice** where the "letter" of the law may be silent, outdated, or inhumane. Eg- Civil disobedience movement by **Gandhiji**

2. **Addressing the "Legal Vacuum"-** Laws cannot predict every future scenario. In these situations, conscience is the only navigator.

1. **Humanizing**

**Bureaucracy-** While rules are rigid, conscience provides the empathy needed for substantive justice.

2. **Upholding Scientific Responsibility-** Eg- **Robert Oppenheimer** faced crisis of conscience after the atomic bomb's use, leading him to advocate for international nuclear control.

3. **Whistleblowing against Corporate Fraud (moral courage) -** Eg- **Sherron Watkins** exposed the Enron scandal.

4. **Guide for social and legal reforms-** Eg- **Martin Luther King’s** fight for Civil Rights Act.

5. **Environmental Stewardship-** Laws often lag behind ecological destruction. Eg- **Sunderlal Bahuguna** led the Chipko Movement based on a conscience-driven duty to protect nature.

6. **Integrity in Sports (**"fair play") - Eg- Sachin Tendulkar walking off before the umpire gives a decision.

7. **Handling Political Pressure-** Eg- **Ashok Khemka** has prioritized his conscience over political convenience, resulting in over 50 transfers.

8. In **conflict zones** where local laws are absent, conscience remains the sole guide. Eg- **Malala Yousafzai**’s campaign for girls' education despite Taliban ban.

9. **Prevents moral blindness**. Eg- Oskar Schindler saved Jews despite Nazi laws.

### Challenges with conscience as a guide

1. **Subjectivity**. Eg- Extremists justify violence as moral duty.

2. **Cultural bias**. Eg- Honour killings defended as conscience by communities.

3. **Moral relativism**. Eg- Different consciences produce conflicting actions.

4. **Conflict with rule of law**. Eg- Mob lynching violates “justice as fairness”

5. **Inconsistency**- Personal morals change across time and contexts.

6. **The "Slippery Slope"-** Once an official starts bypassing rules for "good" reasons, it becomes easier to bypass them for "convenient" or "bad" reasons later.

### Synthesis of Law and Conscience

1. **Constitutional morality as the bridge** - Align personal conscience with constitutional values like justice, liberty and equality.

2. **Ethics training for public officials** under Mission **Karmayogi.**

3. Updation of laws with evolving ethical standards. Eg- DPDP Act

4. **Discretion** should be subject to transparency and review.

5. **Institutional ethical safeguards** - Ethics committees, ombudsmen and judicial oversight The synthesis of the two can create a "Compassionate State."
