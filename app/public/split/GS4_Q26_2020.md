## Question 26 (Year: 2020 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2020-gs4-q26-civilsdaily**

**2020 - A positive attitude is considered to be an essential characteristic of a civil servant who is often required to function under extreme stress. What contributes to a positive attitude in a person?** [Year: 2020] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

"A man is but the product of his thoughts; what he thinks, he becomes." - **Mahatma Gandhi**

In public administration, a **positive attitude** is a mental and emotional mindset that focuses on constructive solutions, resilience, and optimism, even when faced with systemic constraints.

| **Extreme Stress** | **Role of Positive attitude** |

| --- | --- |

| **Crisis management** | **Objectivity** in decision making. Eg- T. N. Seshan |

| **Political pressure** - Eg- Transfer threats. | **Integrity and EI** |

| **Public scrutiny** | **Citizen centrality** to build public trust |

| **Law and order tensions** - Eg- Riots and protests | **Leading from the front for high team morale** |

| **Resource scarcity** | **Innovative problem solving.** Eg-crowdfunding |

| **Conflicting stakeholder interests**. Eg-
Farmers vs industry land disputes. | **Empathy and calmness** to de-escalate tensions |

| **Moral dilemmas** - Eg- Truth vs Loyalty. | Ensuring **ethical consistency** |

| **Work-Life Imbalance** | **Endurance for** preventing "Burnout" |

**Developing a positive attitude is a process of "psychological conditioning" involving several factors-**

1. **Detachment (Nishkama Karma)-** Focus on the process, not just the result.

2. **Moral Clarity (Sense of Mission)-** Viewing work through the lens of a "higher calling." Eg-Commitment to public welfare.

3. **Emotional Intelligence (EQ)-** Recognizing and managing one’s own triggers through **‘mindfulness techniques’** like **"Box Breathing"** (4-4-4-4 technique)

4. **Cognitive Reframing-** Viewing challenges as opportunities. Eg- Seeing a **punishment posting** as a chance to reform.

5. **Physical Well-being-** A healthy body serves as the "hardware" that supports the "software" of a positive mind.

6. **Social Support Systems (The "Ventilator" Effect)-** Eg- Spending quality time with **family or hobby groups** to decompress from official stress.

7. **Competence** and confidence is born from knowledge. Eg- domain specialisation among civil servants (Hota Committee)

8. **Gratitude and "Small Wins" -** Acknowledging incremental progress helps maintain morale when the "big picture" looks bleak.

9. **Intellectual Curiosity and Continuous Learning -** A mind that is constantly learning perceives "change" as exciting rather than threatening.

Thus, a positive attitude transforms a bureaucrat from a **"cog in the machine" into a leader of change.**
