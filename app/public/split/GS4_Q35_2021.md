## Question 35 (Year: 2021 | Paper: GS IV)

**2021 - Should impartial and being non-partisan be considered as indispensable qualities to make a successful civil servant? Discuss with illustrations. [Year: 2021] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

A civil servant derives legitimacy not from popular mandate but from the **trust of neutrality, fairness and rule-based conduct.
**Impartiality is the quality of making decisions based on objective criteria, merit, and facts.
**Non-partisanship is a state of being politically neutral and serving the government with equal dedication, regardless of personal political leanings.
### **Importance of Impartiality
1. Impartiality ensures that **all citizens are treated equally regardless of status, identity or political affiliation. Eg- Kiran Bedi towing vehicle of PM
1. Maintaining **Public Trust and belief in fairness of administration - strengthens legitimacy. Eg- UPSC selection process
1. Promotes inclusivity and Social Justice- Eg- **S. Shankaran in implementing the **Bonded Labour Abolition Act
1. Prevents misuse of power. Eg- **Ashok Khemka (IAS) cancelled illegal land deals irrespective of political interests.
1. Enables fair grievance redressal. Eg- **U Sagayam (IAS) conducted impartial inquiries into granite mining.
1. Ensures **efficiency in resource utilization. Eg- **E. Sreedharan ensuring objectivity in the awarding of contracts for the Delhi Metro.
1. Promotes **professional ethics by ensuring adherence to principles of neutrality, selflessness and accountability.
### **Importance of Non-partisanship
1. Continuity of Government- Eg- The smooth transition of power in India since independence
1. Fearless Advice on the legality of a political directive.
1. Efficient Crisis Management- In communal or ethnic tensions, an impartial officer is the only trusted mediator.
1. It prevents the **"cooking of books" (like GDP or employment figures) to suit a political party's re-election campaign.
1. Prevents politicisation of bureaucracy. Eg- **Durga Shakti Nagpal resisting political interference.
1. Maintains professional autonomy. Eg- RBI independence in monetary policy.
1. Prevents **authoritarian tendencies and ‘spoils system.’
1. Safeguards **rule of law by resisting politically motivated actions. Eg- EC’s fair application of MCC
Thus, impartiality and non-partisanship are **cardinal virtues for preserving **democratic fairness as well as **institutional and ethical integrity.
