## Question 69 (Year: 2024 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2024-gs4-q69-civilsdaily**

**2024 - The application of Artificial Intelligence as a dependable source of input for administrative rational decision-making is a debatable issue. Critically examine the statement from the ethical point of view.** [Year: 2024] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

The integration of AI into administration marks a shift from **"Human Discretion"** to **"Algorithmic Governance."** However, its role as a "dependable source" is **ethically complex** as "rational" decision in administration is not just about **logical consistency**, but about **justice and equity.**

**Ethical Case for AI as a Dependable Source**

1. It reduces **cognitive bias** and offers **Neutrality and Objectivity** in decision making. Eg- use of AI in SSC exam evaluation

2. **Data-driven policymaking** promotes evidence-based governance. Eg- Aarogya Setu App

3. **Ensures consistency and predictability -** Eg- AI based custom approval process by DGFT

4. **Efficiency** in service delivery. Eg- **Samagra Vedika** platform of telangana create a "360-degree profile" of every citizen to verify eligibility for welfare schemes

5. By removing the **"human interface"**, AI reduces the opportunity for **bribery and rent-seeking.** EgAI-driven **MCA21 V3** portal flags suspicious shell companies and fraudulent filings.

6. **Enhances accuracy**. Eg- IMD using AI for weather forcasting

7. **Disaster Management Efficiency-** Eg- **Google's AI Flood Forecasting** model, used by **CWC**, provides real-time flood alerts

8. **Targeted welfare delivery** - AI improves beneficiary identification in DBT.

9. **Utilitarian Optimization- Eg- NITI Aayog** partnered with IBM to develop AI models for **Crop Yield Prediction for farmers**

10. Enhancing **public participation** through **language inclusivity**. Eg- **BHASHINI** platform

**Ethical Concerns**

1. **The "Black Box" Problem-** AI logic is often opaque.

2. **Institutionalizing Historical Bias-** If data is biased , the AI will "learn" and automate that bias. Eg- **US COMPAS tool biased against African-Americans.**

3. **Difficulty in ensuring accountability** for mistakes.

4. **Loss of "Compassion" and "Conscience"-** Eg- It cannot "bend the rules" for a starving widow whose paperwork is missing.

5. **Digital Dehumanization-** AI reduces complex human lives into "risk scores" or "eligibility percentages".

6. **Threat to Right to Privacy-** Constant AI monitoring can turn a "Welfare State" into a "Surveillance State".

7. **Skill Atrophy-** Administrators may stop using their own judgment, leading to a loss of critical thinking -"Steel Frame" to **"Silicon Frame"**

8. Widening **North-South divide** - Poor countries lack access to tech-mediated services.

### Way Forward- "Human-Centric AI."

1. **Explainable AI-** Implementing systems where the AI must provide a "human-readable" reason for every decision.

2. **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)-** The final "sign-off," especially in cases affecting human rights, must be by a human officer.

3. Mandating **regular 3rd-party audits** of government algorithms to detect and "unlearn" biases.

4. **The EU AI Act Approach- "Risk-Based Framework"** where high-risk AI (policing or judiciary) face the highest level of ethical regulation.

5. **Digital Ethics Commissions** including ethicists, jurists, and technologists to oversee AI deployment in public service.

6. **Right to Appeal against AI-** Statutory rights for citizens to have an AI-driven decision reviewed by a human committee.

7. **Ethical Coding Standards-** Teaching **"Ethics by Design"** to programmers working on public infrastructure.

8. Training civil servants in **AI Literacy** under **Mission Karmayogi**.

9. Strengthening **privacy safeguards under DPDP Act.**

The goal must be not AI-driven governance, but **ethically guided AI-assisted governance**.
