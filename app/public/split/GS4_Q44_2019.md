## Question 44 (Year: 2019 | Paper: GS IV)

**2019 - “Non-performance of duty by a public servant is a form of corruption”. Do you agree with this view? Justify your answer. [Year: 2019] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

“The greatest corruption is the corruption of duty.” - **Edmund Burke
Conventionally, corruption is viewed as the "misuse of public office for private gain," however, in a broader ethical sense, **omission (failing to act) is as damaging as **commission (acting wrongly).
**Non-performance of duty as form of corruption 1. Breach of the "Social Contract" - The public pays taxes in exchange for services. Non-performance is a unilateral breach of this trust.
projects
1. Inaction is a betrayal of the **"fiduciary duty" owed to the citizens. Eg- Police officers failing to register an FIR in SC, ST atrocity cases.
1. Wastage of Resources- Eg- food grains roting in **FCI godowns while tribal populations suffer from malnutrition.
1. When officials don't work, citizens **lose faith in democracy and turn to **extra-constitutional means for justice. Eg- **rise of "vigilante justice"
### Moral hazard - Inaction normalises irresponsibility.
1. Facilitates exploitation. Eg- Labour inspectors not enforcing minimum wage laws.
### **Ways to Curb Corruption and Non-Performance
1. Code of Ethics & Code of Conduct. (2nd ARC)
1. Implementation of Citizens’ Charters- Eg- SAKALA (Karnataka Guarantee of Services Act)
1. Giving legal backing to **Social Audits
1. Leveraging Technology. Eg- Faceless Income Tax Assessment
1. Strengthening the **Lokpal and Lokayuktas to investigate "maladministration" and "neglect"
1. Independent Commission Against Corruption - "Hong Kong Model"
1. **7. Performance-linked accountability. Eg- 360 degree feedback
A culture of **active responsibility, diligence and accountability is needed to deal with the menace of corruption.
