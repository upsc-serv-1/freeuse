## Question 74 (Year: 2023 | Paper: GS IV)

**2023 - What do you understand by ‘moral integrity’ and ‘professional efficiency in the context of corporate governance in India ? Illustrate with suitable examples. [Year: 2023] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

**Corporate Governance refers to the system of rules, practices and processes by which companies are directed and controlled to balance the interests of all stakeholders.
### **Moral Integrity
It refers to the consistent adherence to ethical principles, honesty, and truthfulness in all business dealings.
1. Transparency- Being open about financial and operational health.
1. Accountability- Taking responsibility for actions and failures.
1. Whistleblowing Support- Encouraging the reporting of internal fraud.
1. Fairness- Eg- **SEBI’s "Related Party Transaction" rules to prevent promoters from siphoning funds to their own private entities.
### Probity - Avoidance of impropriety and unethical conduct.
1. Truthfulness in Marketing- Not misleading the public about products.
1. Trusteeship- Viewing wealth as a social trust. Eg- **Tata Group- 66% equity held by philanthropic trusts.
1. Conflict of Interest Management- Keeping personal and professional roles separate.
1. Environmental Stewardship- Eg- **Godrej Consumer Products achieving "Plastic Neutrality" ahead of government mandates.
**Example - Tanishq’s **"Karatmeter" machine - allow customers to check the purity of their gold for free.
### **Professional Efficiency
**It is the competency and capability of the management to achieve maximum output (profits/value) with minimum waste (capital/time).
1. Prudent Capital Allocation- Investing money where it generates the highest returns. Eg- **Asian Paints consistently delivering high RoI through investment in supply chains.
1. Risk Management- Identifying and mitigating financial threats. Eg- **Kotak Mahindra Bank- efficient lending practices.
1. Technological Agility- Adapting to digital trends to stay relevant.
1. Supply Chain Excellence- Eg- **Maruti Suzuki-pioneering "Just-in-Time" (JIT) manufacturing in India to reduce inventory costs.
1. Human Resource Optimization- Training and retaining high-quality talent.
1. Strategic Foresight- Predicting future market shifts. Eg- **Mahindra & Mahindra shift toward EVs and SUVs.
1. Cost efficiency- Producing quality goods at the most competitive price. Eg- Indian IT sector
1. Adherence to Timelines- Delivering projects without delays.
1. Customer-Centric Innovation for improving user experience
**Example - **Maruti Suzuki’s adoption of Japanese lean production systems improved productivity, quality and cost efficiency in Indian automobile manufacturing.
