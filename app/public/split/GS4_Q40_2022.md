### Answer

1. Constitutional Morality - Adherence to the core philosophies of the Constitution even when they clash with popular social norms or traditional beliefs.
**Key pillars
1. a. Rule of Law - Eg- Article 14
1. b. Institutional Integrity - Eg- autonomy of EC, CAG
1. c. Progressive Interpretation - Eg- right to privacy
1. d. Equality and Dignity - Eg- Sabrimala Judgment
1. e. Constitutional Values - Upholding justice, liberty, equality, fraternity
1. f. Separation of Powers and **Checks and Balances
1. Conflict of Interest - A situation where a public official’s private interests could improperly influence their official decisions. Eg- **Chanda Kochar case
**Reasons
1. a. Dual roles - official and private interests overlapping
1. b. Post-retirement employment expectations (revolving door)
1. c. Political affiliations or ideological bias
1. d. Gifts, hospitality and favours from stakeholders
1. e. Weak disclosure and transparency mechanisms
1. f. Discretionary power
1. g. Lack of ethical awareness and training
1. Probity in Public Life - The quality of having strong moral principles, including absolute honesty, transparency, and the active application of integrity in every administrative action. **Eg- U. Sagayam (IAS) - 1st IAS office to declare his personal assets
Key pillars
1. a. Integrity and Honesty
1. b. Transparency and accountability
1. c. Impartiality and objectivity
1. d. Ethical leadership
