## Question 9 (Year: 2019 | Paper: GS IV)

**2019 - What is meant by the term ‘constitutional morality’? How does one uphold constitutional morality? [Year: 2019] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

**Constitutional morality implies adherence to the **core principles and spirit of the Constitution in a democracy. In the words of **Ambedkar, “Constitutional morality is not a natural sentiment. It has to be cultivated”.
**George Grote, in his **History of Greece, described constitutional morality as a **“rare and difficult sentiment” involving
1. Paramount reverence for **forms and procedures of the Constitution.
1. Adherence to law while enabling **open criticism of authority.
1. Need for **public reason, self-restraint, and trust in institutions.
### **Other Pillars
### Rule of Law - Eg- Article 14
### Institutional Integrity - Eg- autonomy of EC, CAG
### Progressive Interpretation - Eg- right to privacy
### Equality and Dignity - Eg- Sabrimala Judgment
1. Constitutional Values - Upholding justice, liberty, equality, fraternity
1. Separation of Powers and **Checks and Balances
**Upholding constitutional morality
1. **1. By Judiciary
1. ** Prioritizing Constitutional over Social Morality- Eg- Navtej Singh Johar case
1. ** Protecting Dissent and Liberty- Eg- upholding "Bail is the rule, Jail is the exception."
1. ** Ensuring procedural fairness - Following due process and natural justice.
1. **2. By Legislature
1. ** Debate and Deliberation- Eg- mandatory referral of bill to Parliamentary committee (UK Model)
1. ** Inclusive Law-making to protect the interests of the marginalized. Eg- **Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016
1. **• Ensuring representation - Eg- Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam, 2023
1. ** Social Reform through Legislation to rectify historical injustices. Eg- Triple Talaq Act
1. **3. The Executive
1. ** Self-Restraint in Power- Eg- Following the **S.R. Bommai guidelines to avoid the unnecessary imposition of President’s Rule (Article 356).
1. ** Adherence to the "Rule of Law"- Eg- Strictly following the **D.K. Basu guidelines to prevent custodial torture.
1. **4. Public Administration
1. ** Anonymity and Neutrality- Providing "fair and fearless" advice to ministers
1. ** Practicing Compassion- Using "administrative discretion" to help the last person in the queue (Antyodaya). Eg- **S. Shankaran in implementing the **Bonded Labour Abolition Act
1. ** Transparency and Honesty- Proactive disclosure under the **RTI Act.
1. **5. By Citizens (The Sovereign)
1. ** Tolerance of Dissent- Respecting the right of others to have a different opinion, religion, or lifestyle.
1. ** Performing Fundamental Duties- Recognizing that rights come with responsibilities (Article 51A).
1. ** Active Participation- Eg- Participating in **Gram Sabhas or city ward committee meetings Constitutional morality serves as the **"North Star," ensuring that the ship of the state remains anchored in the values of human dignity and social justice.
