## Question 54 (Year: 2024 | Paper: GS IV)

**2024 - “Mindless addiction to Form, ignoring the Substance of the matter, results in rendering of injustice. A perceptive civil servant is one who ignores such literalness and carries out true intent.” Examine the above statement with suitable illustrations. [Year: 2024] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

In public administration, there is tension between **procedural formalism and **substantive justice. When it becomes a slave to "Form," it becomes an end in itself, at the cost of the citizen it was meant to serve.
### **Mindless Addiction to Form- Rendering Injustice
1. Institutionalization of Red Tapism- Eg- **Munger-Ganga Bridge in Bihar was delayed by nearly 14 years due to inter-departmental disputes
1. Literalness breeds a **"mechanical" mindset where officials **lose empathy. Eg- denial of emergency care to critically ill patient without documentation
1. Inefficiency in crisis management- Eg- migrant labour sufferings during COVID.
1. Undermining Rights of the Vulnerable- Eg- PDS exclusion of poor due to biometric authentication failures (Jharkhand case)
1. Encourages moral abdication - Officials transfer moral responsibility to the rulebook (banality of evil). Eg- encroachment demolitions without rehabilitation plan.
1. Facilitates **coercive corruption. Eg- ‘speed money’ for faster file clearance
1. Complexity of the "Form" leads to **Process becoming Punishment. Eg- complexity in environmental clearances
1. "By-the-book" culture **stifles innovation and creativity.
1. Resource Wastage- Eg- cost and time overruns in PPP projects due to delays in land procurement
1. Policy Paralysis- Fear of audit or the "3C" (CBI, CVC, CAG) leads to "Defensive Bureaucracy."
### **The Perceptive Civil Servant- Carrying Out True Intent
1. Prioritizing Humanitarian Spirit and constitutional morality. Eg- **Dr. Syed Sehrish Asgar used her office as a "human call center" during communication blackouts in J&K
1. Proactive Problem-Solving in emergencies- Eg-Dr. Rajendra Bharud (IAS) setting up an oxygen plant in tribal Nandurbar before the second COVID wave
1. Empowering through Informal Networks- Eg**Armstrong Pame (IAS) built a 100km road via crowdfunding.
1. Administrative Empathy for the Marginalized- EgIn the **"Project Sulaimani" model (Kozhikode), the "Substance" of dignity was prioritized.
1. Reforming through Discretion- Eg- **Kiran Bedi (IPS) turned Tihar Jail into a reformatory.
1. Streamlining Processes for the Citizen- Perceptive officers redesign the "Form" itself. **DM of Ganjam (Vijay Kulange) institutionalized "Doorstep Delivery" of pensions for the disabled
1. Innovative problem solving- Eg- **Rahul Kumar (IAS) converted abandoned buildings into libraries using maintenance funds.
1. Defending the Rights of the Voiceless- Eg- **S. Sankaran (IAS) provided instant subsistence relief to rescued bonded laborers.
Rules are meant to serve justice and when they fail to do so, conscience must guide discretion.
