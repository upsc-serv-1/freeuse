## Question 54 (Year: 2024 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2024-gs4-q54-civilsdaily**

**2024 - “Mindless addiction to Form, ignoring the Substance of the matter, results in rendering of injustice. A perceptive civil servant is one who ignores such literalness and carries out true intent.” Examine the above statement with suitable illustrations.** [Year: 2024] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

In public administration, there is tension between **procedural formalism **and **substantive justice. When it becomes a slave to "Form," it becomes an end in itself, at the cost of the citizen it was meant to serve.

### Mindless Addiction to Form- Rendering Injustice

1.** Institutionalization of Red Tapism- **Eg- **Munger-Ganga Bridge **in Bihar was delayed by nearly 14 years due to inter-departmental disputes

2. Literalness breeds a **"mechanical" mindset **where officials **lose empathy. **Eg- denial of emergency care to critically ill patient without documentation

3.** Inefficiency in crisis management- **Eg- migrant labour sufferings during COVID.

4.** Undermining Rights of the Vulnerable- **Eg- PDS exclusion of poor due to biometric authentication failures (Jharkhand case)

5.** Encourages moral abdication - **Officials transfer moral responsibility to the rulebook (banality of evil). Eg- encroachment demolitions without rehabilitation plan.

6. Facilitates **coercive corruption. Eg- ‘speed money’ for faster file clearance

7. Complexity of the "Form" leads to **Process becoming Punishment. Eg- **complexity in environmental clearances

8. "By-the-book" culture **stifles innovation and creativity.**

9.** Resource Wastage- **Eg- cost and time overruns in PPP projects due to delays in land procurement

10.** Policy Paralysis- **Fear of audit or the "3C" (CBI, CVC, CAG) leads to "Defensive Bureaucracy."

### The Perceptive Civil Servant- Carrying Out True Intent

1.** Prioritizing Humanitarian Spirit and constitutional morality. **Eg- **Dr. Syed Sehrish Asgar **used her office as a "human call center" during communication blackouts in J&K

2.** Proactive Problem-Solving in emergencies- **Eg-**Dr. Rajendra Bharud (IAS) **setting up an oxygen plant in tribal Nandurbar before the second COVID wave

3.** Empowering through Informal Networks- **Eg**Armstrong Pame (IAS) **built a 100km road via crowdfunding.

4.** Administrative Empathy for the Marginalized- **EgIn the **"Project Sulaimani" **model (Kozhikode), the "Substance" of dignity was prioritized.

5.** Reforming through Discretion- **Eg- **Kiran Bedi (IPS) **turned Tihar Jail into a reformatory.

6.** Streamlining Processes for the Citizen- **Perceptive officers redesign the "Form" itself. **DM of Ganjam (Vijay Kulange) **institutionalized "Doorstep Delivery" of pensions for the disabled

7.** Innovative problem solving- **Eg- **Rahul Kumar (IAS) **converted abandoned buildings into libraries using maintenance funds.

8.** Defending the Rights of the Voiceless- **Eg- **S. Sankaran (IAS) **provided instant subsistence relief to rescued bonded laborers.

Rules are meant to serve justice and when they fail to do so, conscience must guide discretion.
