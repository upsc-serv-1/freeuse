**2023 - Explain the term social capital. How does it enhance good governance?** [Year: 2023] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

**Social capital **refers to the **networks, norms, values and trust **that enable individuals and groups to cooperate for mutual benefit and collective action.

"Social capital is the lubricant that makes the wheels of democracy turn."

According to political scientist **Robert Putnam, social capital consists of two main types-

- ** Bonding Social Capital- **Links between people of similar backgrounds (family, class).

- ** Bridging Social Capital- **Links between people from different groups.

### Role of Social Capital in Enhancing Good Governance

1.** Enhanced Accountability- **High social capital empowers citizens to monitor the government. Eg- high public participation in local government in kerala

2.** Reduction in Transaction Costs- **When trust is high, the government spends less on policing and litigation.

3.** Facilitating "Co-Production" of Services- **Eg- The **Bhagidari Scheme **in

Delhi involved RWAs in managing local water and electricity issues.

4.** Effective Grievance Redressal- **Strong social networks **amplify the voice of the marginalized. **Eg- **Kudumbashree **network in Kerala.

5.** Promoting "Bottom-Up" Planning- **Social capital makes the **Gram Sabha **a vibrant decision-making body. Eg- **people’s plan campaign in kerala**

6.** Reducing Conflict and Promoting Peace- **Eg- **Peace Committees **use inter-community trust to de-escalate tensions before they turn into riots.

7.** Behavioral Change (Nudge Theory)- **Social capital is the engine of **"Jan Andolan" **(People's Movements). Eg- success of **Pulse Polio **immunization.

8.** Improving Public Policy Compliance- **Eg- Higher **tax compliance **rates in countries where citizens perceive the "social contract" to be strong and fair.

9.** Inclusive Resource Management- **It prevents the "Tragedy of the Commons."

10.** Humanizing the Bureaucracy- **It bridges the gap between the "Ruler" and the "Ruled." Eg-**"Compassionate Kozhikode" **initiative by IAS Prasant Nair

**Ways to Build social capital**

1. Strengthening Grassroots Decentralization with 4F’s

2. Education for "Civic Virtue"

3. Institutionalizing Community Policing

4. Strengthening civil society as a “bridge”

5. Implementing "Social Audits" as a Norm

For India to move toward **"Minimum Government, Maximum Governance," **the state must act as a facilitator of social capital, transforming passive subjects into active, trusting, and responsible citizens.
