## Question 17 (Year: 2024 | Paper: GS IV)

**2024 - Global warming and climate change are the outcomes of human greed in the name of development, indicating the direction in which extinction of organisms including human beings is heading towards loss of life on Earth. How do you put an end to this to protect life and bring equilibrium between the society and the environment? [Year: 2024] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

The crisis of climate change is fundamentally a **crisis of values. It is the product of pursuing **material progress by disregarding **planetary boundaries.
"Earth provides enough to satisfy every man's needs, but not every man's greed." - **Mahatma
**Gandhi
**Human Greed
**Ecological & Biological Impact
**Fossil Fuel addiction
Massive CO2 emissions (**38.1 billion tonnes in 2025)
**Deforestation for development
Habitat loss for 80% of terrestrial species.
**Industrial Agriculture
Soil degradation and the "insect apocalypse"
**Fast Fashion & Consumerism
microplastic pollution
**Illegal Wildlife Trade
increased risk of zoonotic pandemics.
**Overfishing
Collapse of marine biodiversity and "dead zones"
**Urban Sprawl
Heat island effects
**Ways to Bring Equilibrium Between Society and Environment
**Global Level- Systemic Harmonization
1. ** Decarbonization of Global Trade-
Eg- taxing products imported from high-pollution regions
1. ** Climate Finance MobilizationFulfilling the $100 billion annual commitment by developed countries
1. ** Protection of global commons - Eg-early finalization of Global Plastic Treaty to protect oceans **Government Level- act as the "Architect of Sustainability"
1. ** Rights of Nature Legislation-
Formally granting legal personhood to
major rivers, mountains, and forests. Eg- Ganga in india
1. ** Energy Transition under **Panchamrit Targets- 500 GW from non-fossil sources by 2030.
1. ** Green Urban Planning- Developing "Sponge Cities" with permeable pavements and urban wetlands to manage flooding and "Heat Island" effects.
### **Business Level
1. ** Adopting Net-Zero Roadmaps- Measuring and reducing **Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions
1. ** Product Stewardship- Designing products that can be easily repaired or recycled
1. ** ESG Integration in investment decisions, recognizing that "Sustainability is a Competitive Advantage."
1. ** Supply Chain Decarbonization- ensuring that raw materials are sourced ethically and without deforestation.
1. ** Bio-Inclusive Boardrooms- Eg- "Ecological Representative" or "Voice for Future Generations" on corporate boards
### **Society Level- Cultural Re-engineering
1. ** Mainstreaming "Environmentalism of the Poor"- Eg- **Chipko or **Appico movements where local tribes are the primary guardians of forests.
1. Moving from "Environmental Science" to **"Environmental Ethics" in school **and college curricula
1. ** Community-Led Restoration- Eg- volunteer movements for cleaning coastlines or planting "Miyawaki" urban forests.
### **Citizen Level- LiFE (Lifestyle for Environment) movement
1. ** Mindful Consumption through **ethical restraint
1. a. Switching from a "throwaway culture" to one of repair and reuse
1. b. lifestyle of **"Enoughness"-voluntarily limiting consumption
1. c. "Pro-Planet" dietary Shift to protect water and soil. Eg- millet-based diets
1. ** Energy Citizenship- Transitioning to rooftop solar and adopting energy-efficient appliances (LEDs/BLDC fans).
1. ** Waste Segregation at Source- helps 100% composting or recycling at the municipal level.
1. ** Green Mobility- Preferring public transport, cycling, or Electric Vehicles (EVs)
By applying the **Precautionary Principle to our technology and **Intergenerational Equity to our economy, we can move to the trajectory of **"Great Restoration."
