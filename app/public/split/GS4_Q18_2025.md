## Question 18 (Year: 2025 | Paper: GS IV)

**2025 - Keeping the national security in mind, examine the ethical dilemmas related to controversies over environmental clearance of development projects in ecologically sensitive border areas in the country. [Year: 2025] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

While national security is a **non-negotiable sovereign duty, ecological health is a **non-negotiable biological necessity. The ethical dilemma arises because both sides claim the "Right to Life" (Article 21).
### **National security imperative
1. All weather connectivity. Eg- **Zojila or **Sela tunnels
1. Logistics and supply lines
1. Surveillance and communication. Eg- Installing radar stations
1. Rapid deployment capability
1. Strategic deterrence. Eg- Great Nicobar Port in IOR
1. Disaster response access
1. Hearts and minds strategy. Eg- vibrant village program
### **Ethical dilemmas related to environmental clearance
1. Utilitarianism vs. Deontology/ right vs right dilemma - sacrificing a unique, irreplaceable ecosystem for the "greater good" of national security.
1. Connectivity vs ecology - Highway projects degrade fragile ecosystems. Eg- Char Dham Pariyojana
1. Anthropocentrism vs. Ecocentrism- Does a border road have more "value" than the extinction of an endemic species?
1. National interest vs local rights - Eg- **Forest Conservation (Amendment) Act 2023 exempts projects within 100km of borders from certain clearances.
### Speed vs scrutiny - Eg- Strategic exemptions from EIA.
1. Secrecy vs transparency - Classified projects limit public accountability.
1. Intergenerational justice dilemma - Immediate defence gains create long-term ecological vulnerability. Eg- Landslides due to hill cutting.
1. Human safety vs wildlife protection - Eg- Border fencing affecting elephant movement.
1. Development vs sustainability - Infrastructure improves access but undermines long-term ecological balance.
1. Slippery Slope dilemma - extension of ‘national security exemptions’ to other projects in future
Case studies
### Great Nicobar Project - Loss of **130 sq. km of rainforest
1. Arunachal Frontier Highway - Fragmentation of the **Dibang Valley
1. Zojila Tunnel - Disruption of **permafrost and glacial hydrology
### **Way Forward
1. ** Strategic Environmental Assessment (SEA)
1. ** Green Engineering. Eg- "Eco-bridges"
1. ** Independent Oversight Body comprising both military strategists and ecologists
1. ** Restorative Compensation
There is need to shift from "Environmental Clearance" to **"Environmental Integration" for sustainable and **"Sensitive” security paradigm.
