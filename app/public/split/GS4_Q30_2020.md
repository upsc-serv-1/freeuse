## Question 30 (Year: 2020 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2020-gs4-q30-civilsdaily**

**2020 - What are the main components of emotional intelligence (EI)? Can they be learned? Discuss.** [Year: 2020] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

**Emotional Intelligence** is the capacity to recognise, understand, manage and use emotions constructively in oneself and in others.

“When awareness is brought to emotion, power is brought to your life.” - **Daniel Goleman**

### Main Components of Emotional Intelligence

**1. Self-Awareness -**

    a. Involves recognizing your own emotions, strengths, weaknesses, and triggers in real-time.

    b. **Abraham Lincoln** recognised his "melancholy" and managed through humor, storytelling, and developing immense empathy

**2. Self-Regulation -**

    a. Involves managing and redirecting disruptive emotions and impulses.

    b. It prevents "Amygdala Hijack"-where the emotional brain takes over the rational brain.

    c. Eg- **Captain Sullenberger** - Regulated fear during engine failure to land safely on Hudson.

    d. **Warren Buffett’s emotional detachment** strategy - stay greedy when others are fearful

**3. Motivation-**

    a. Inner drive to pursue goals with energy and persistence

    b. **Eg- Marie Curie** worked in a shed for years to isolate radium.

    c. **Viktor Frankl** survived the Holocaust by finding "meaning" in his suffering. (**Logotherapy**)

**4. Empathy -**

    a. Ability to understand the emotions and perspectives of others.

    b. Essential for cross-cultural communication and building trust within a team.

    c. Eg- **Mahatma Gandhi-** lived like poor to "feel" their struggle.

**5. Social Skills -**

    a. Ability to manage relationships, build networks, resolve conflicts and inspire others.

    b. **Satish Dhawan (ISRO)** taking blame for SLV-3 mission failure

    c. Arif Sheikh leading “amcho police, amcho baster”

Unlike IQ, which is largely fixed by early adulthood, EI is based on **Neuroplasticity** - the brain's ability to form new neural pathways through repeated behavior. It can be learned through

### Self-Awareness

1. **Reflection and self-observation** through **mindfulness and meditation**. Eg- Vipassana.

2. **Reflective journaling** - Writing daily emotional experiences and triggers.

3. **360-degree feedback** from peers and seniors to reveal blind spots.

### Self-Regulation

1. **Stress-management training** to calm emotional arousal. Eg- "crisis-room" simulation in training

2. **Cognitive Reframing-** Viewing challenges as opportunities. Eg- Seeing a **punishment posting** as a chance to reform.

3. **Delay techniques** - Eg- 10-second rule before responding in anger.

4. **Physical regulation** - Exercise, sleep and breathing control helps in emotional stability.

### Motivation

1. Defining meaningful **personal and professional goals.** Eg- SMART goal frameworks.

2. **Positive psychology practices** -Eg- Writing three positive outcomes daily.

3. **Role Modelling**. Eg- **"Technocratic Optimism"** of E. Sreedharan for a solution-oriented attitude

### Empathy

1. **Perspective-taking exercises** enhance empathy and understanding. Eg- **village immersion** programs

2. **Direct citizen interaction** exposes civil servants **to diversity** - develop compassion and leadership. Eg- Jan Sunwais

### Social Skills

1. **Communication skills training**. Eg- Public speaking workshops.

2. **Life experiences** gradually deepen emotional understanding.

Thus, Emotional Intelligence is not innate or fixed. It is **learnable, trainable and improvable.**
