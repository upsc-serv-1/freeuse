## Question 30 (Year: 2020 | Paper: GS IV)

**2020 - What are the main components of emotional intelligence (EI)? Can they be learned? Discuss. [Year: 2020] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

**Emotional Intelligence is the capacity to recognise, understand, manage and use emotions constructively in oneself and in others.
“When awareness is brought to emotion, power is brought to your life.” - **Daniel Goleman
### **Main Components of Emotional Intelligence
1. **1. Self-Awareness -
1. a. Involves recognizing your own emotions, strengths, weaknesses, and triggers in real-time.
1. b. Abraham Lincoln recognised his "melancholy" and managed through humor, storytelling, and developing immense empathy
1. **2. Self-Regulation -
1. a. Involves managing and redirecting disruptive emotions and impulses.
1. b. It prevents "Amygdala Hijack"-where the emotional brain takes over the rational brain.
1. c. Eg- **Captain Sullenberger - Regulated fear during engine failure to land safely on Hudson.
1. d. Warren Buffett’s emotional detachment strategy - stay greedy when others are fearful
1. **3. Motivation-
1. a. Inner drive to pursue goals with energy and persistence
1. b. Eg- Marie Curie worked in a shed for years to isolate radium.
1. c. Viktor Frankl survived the Holocaust by finding "meaning" in his suffering. (**Logotherapy)
1. **4. Empathy -
1. a. Ability to understand the emotions and perspectives of others.
1. b. Essential for cross-cultural communication and building trust within a team.
1. c. Eg- **Mahatma Gandhi- lived like poor to "feel" their struggle.
1. **5. Social Skills -
1. a. Ability to manage relationships, build networks, resolve conflicts and inspire others.
1. b. Satish Dhawan (ISRO) taking blame for SLV-3 mission failure
1. c. Arif Sheikh leading “amcho police, amcho baster”
Unlike IQ, which is largely fixed by early adulthood, EI is based on **Neuroplasticity - the brain's ability to form new neural pathways through repeated behavior. It can be learned through
### **Self-Awareness
1. Reflection and self-observation through **mindfulness and meditation. Eg- Vipassana.
1. Reflective journaling - Writing daily emotional experiences and triggers.
1. 360-degree feedback from peers and seniors to reveal blind spots.
### **Self-Regulation
1. Stress-management training to calm emotional arousal. Eg- "crisis-room" simulation in training
1. Cognitive Reframing- Viewing challenges as opportunities. Eg- Seeing a **punishment posting as a chance to reform.
1. Delay techniques - Eg- 10-second rule before responding in anger.
1. Physical regulation - Exercise, sleep and breathing control helps in emotional stability.
### **Motivation
1. Defining meaningful **personal and professional goals. Eg- SMART goal frameworks.
1. Positive psychology practices -Eg- Writing three positive outcomes daily.
1. Role Modelling. Eg- **"Technocratic Optimism" of E. Sreedharan for a solution-oriented attitude
### **Empathy
1. Perspective-taking exercises enhance empathy and understanding. Eg- **village immersion programs
1. Direct citizen interaction exposes civil servants **to diversity - develop compassion and leadership. Eg- Jan Sunwais
### **Social Skills
1. Communication skills training. Eg- Public speaking workshops.
1. Life experiences gradually deepen emotional understanding.
Thus, Emotional Intelligence is not innate or fixed. It is **learnable, trainable and improvable.
