## Question 29 (Year: 2019 | Paper: GS IV)

**2019 - “Emotional Intelligence is the ability to make your emotions work for you instead of against you”. Do you agree with this view? Discuss. [Year: 2019] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

**Emotional Intelligence is the capacity to recognise, understand, manage and use emotions constructively in oneself and in others.
“When awareness is brought to emotion, power is brought to your life.” - **Daniel Goleman
**EI making emotions work for you
1. **1. Self-awareness
1. Recognising emotions before they control behaviour helps manage anger and frustration
1. Understanding emotional triggers helps in better preparation. Eg- Awareness of anxiety before public speaking.
1. Realistic self-assessment helps in knowing one’s strengths and weaknesses.
1. **2. Self-regulation
1. a. **Impulse control - Prevents emotional
outbursts and rash decisions. Eg- Not shouting at subordinates during stress.
1. Ensures emotional balance - Eg- Calm leadership during disasters.
1. Builds adaptability to changing situations. Eg- Accepting e-governance by civil servants
1. **3. Motivation
1. a. Positive drive helps turn frustration into determination. Eg- Officers working overtime during elections to ensure fairness.
1. b. Optimism despite setbacks. Eg- ISRO successfully launching Chandrayan 3 after failure of Chandrayan 2
1. **4. Empathy
1. a. Understanding others’ feelings and viewpoints helps developing **broader outlook and leadership
1. b. Sensitivity to diversity helps civil servants in handling community tensions sensitively.
1. **5. Social skills
1. a. Effective communication by expressing emotions constructively. Eg- PM addressing the nation during a pandemic.
1. b. Relationship building - Eg- **Operation Sadbhavana of Army in J&K for trust and cooperation with locals
### **Counter-arguments- How Emotional Intelligence can make emotions work against you
1. **1. Emotional manipulation - Eg- caste politics by leaders
1. **2. Emotion-reading skills can enable fake empathy and dishonesty.
1. **3. Rationalising wrongdoing. Eg- justifying corruption as “systemic necessity.”
1. **4. Emotional burnout due to constant **emotional regulation
1. High empathy may lead to compromise on objectivity and firmness
1. Conflict avoidance instead of **necessary confrontation.
1. Emotional intelligence without moral compass becomes a tool of self interest
**Thus, EI + Integrity is needed to develop virtues of wisdom and compassion.
