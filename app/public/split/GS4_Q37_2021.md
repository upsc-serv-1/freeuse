## Question 37 (Year: 2021 | Paper: GS IV)

**2021 - Besides domain knowledge, a public official needs innovativeness and creativity of a high order as well, while resolving ethical dilemmas. Discuss with suitable examples. [Year: 2021] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

“In times of complexity, imagination is more important than knowledge.” - **Albert Einstein
Innovativeness and creativity enable officials to find **morally acceptable and practically workable solutions.
### **Ethical Dilemmas in Civil Services
1. Development vs. Displacement- Narmada Bachao Andolan (Medha Patkar vs. State).
1. Confidentiality vs. Transparency- Whistleblowing
1. Political Pressure vs. Neutrality
1. Freedom of Speech vs. Public Order
1. Environment vs. Livelihood
1. Efficiency vs. Equity
1. Personal Ethics vs. Professional Duty
1. Loyalty vs. Truth
### **Importance of Domain Knowledge
1. Policy Formulation- Dr. Manmohan Singh’s economic expertise during the 1991 reforms.
1. Infrastructure Excellence- E. Sreedharan’s engineering mastery in the Delhi Metro.
1. Economic Management- Raghuram Rajan’s knowledge of monetary policy at the RBI.
1. Scientific Progress- Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam’s aerospace knowledge in the Missile Program.
1. Legal Accuracy- T.N. Seshan’s deep understanding of Constitutional Law as CEC.
1. Health Administration- Dr. Gagandeep Kang’s virology expertise during vaccine development.
1. Agricultural Transformation- Dr. M.S. Swaminathan in the Green Revolution.
1. Digital Governance- Nandan Nilekani’s tech background in the creation of Aadhaar.
1. Financial expertise- Vinod Rai’s auditing expertise as the CAG of India.
### **Need for Innovation and Creativity to Resolve Ethical Dilemmas
1. Resource optimization- Eg- **Armstrong Pame (IAS) crowdfunded the "People’s Road".
1. Reformative justice- Kiran Bedi (IPS) introduced Vipassana in Tihar to reform rather than just punish.
1. Prioritising **social welfare by bypassing bureaucratic rigidity. Eg- **Prashant Nair’s "Operation Sulaimani" - Use of crowdsourced coupons to feed hunger
1. Innovative solutions for **winning ‘hearts and minds’ and delegitimizing insurgency. Eg- **Saurabh Kumar (IAS) built "Education City" in Dantewada.
1. Finding **‘out of the box’ solutions. Eg- IPS **chetan singh Rathore using National Anthem to manage protesting crowd.
1. Helps in **effective management of crisis situations. Eg- **Dr. Syed Sehrish Asgar used her office as a "human call center" during communication blackouts in J&K
1. Helps in **collaborative problem solving rather than a top-down approach. Eg- S.R. Sankaran involved local community elders in identifying bonded laborers.
1. Creativity and innovations facilitate adoption of technology to solve problems. Eg- **Dr. Hari Chandana building pavements from recycled plastic in Hyderabad.
1. Can help address **social stigma associated with manual work. **Eg- Ritu Sain (IAS) turned waste-pickers into "Green Commandos".
Ethical governance today requires not just knowledge of rules but **moral imagination, contextual intelligence and creative problem-solving.
