## Question 37 (Year: 2021 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2021-gs4-q37-civilsdaily**

**2021 - Besides domain knowledge, a public official needs innovativeness and creativity of a high order as well, while resolving ethical dilemmas. Discuss with suitable examples.** [Year: 2021] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

“In times of complexity, imagination is more important than knowledge.” - **Albert Einstein**

Innovativeness and creativity enable officials to find **morally acceptable and practically workable** solutions.

### Ethical Dilemmas in Civil Services

1. **Development vs. Displacement-** Narmada Bachao Andolan (Medha Patkar vs. State).

2. **Confidentiality vs. Transparency-** Whistleblowing

3. **Political Pressure vs. Neutrality**

4. **Freedom of Speech vs. Public Order**

5. **Environment vs. Livelihood**

6. **Efficiency vs. Equity**

7. **Personal Ethics vs. Professional Duty**

8. **Loyalty vs. Truth**

### Importance of Domain Knowledge

1. **Policy Formulation- Dr. Manmohan Singh’s** economic expertise during the 1991 reforms.

2. **Infrastructure Excellence- E. Sreedharan’s** engineering mastery in the Delhi Metro.

3. **Economic Management- Raghuram Rajan’s** knowledge of monetary policy at the RBI.

4. **Scientific Progress- Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam’s** aerospace knowledge in the Missile Program.

5. **Legal Accuracy- T.N. Seshan’s** deep understanding of Constitutional Law as CEC.

6. **Health Administration- Dr. Gagandeep Kang’s** virology expertise during vaccine development.

7. **Agricultural Transformation- Dr. M.S. Swaminathan** in the Green Revolution.

8. **Digital Governance- Nandan Nilekani’s** tech background in the creation of Aadhaar.

9. **Financial expertise- Vinod Rai’s** auditing expertise as the CAG of India.

### Need for Innovation and Creativity to Resolve Ethical Dilemmas

1. **Resource optimization-** Eg- **Armstrong Pame (IAS)** crowdfunded the "People’s Road".

2. **Reformative justice- Kiran Bedi (IPS)** introduced Vipassana in Tihar to reform rather than just punish.

3. Prioritising **social welfare** by bypassing bureaucratic rigidity. Eg- **Prashant Nair’s** "Operation Sulaimani" - Use of crowdsourced coupons to feed hunger

4. Innovative solutions for **winning ‘hearts and minds’** and delegitimizing insurgency. Eg- **Saurabh Kumar (IAS)** built "Education City" in Dantewada.

5. Finding **‘out of the box’ solutions**. Eg- IPS **chetan singh Rathore** using National Anthem to manage protesting crowd.

6. Helps in **effective management of crisis situations**. Eg- **Dr. Syed Sehrish Asgar** used her office as a "human call center" during communication blackouts in J&K

7. Helps in **collaborative problem solving** rather than a top-down approach**. Eg- S.R. Sankaran** involved local community elders in identifying bonded laborers.

8. Creativity and innovations facilitate adoption of technology to solve problems. Eg- **Dr. Hari Chandana** building pavements from recycled plastic in Hyderabad.

9. Can help address **social stigma** associated with manual work. **Eg- Ritu Sain (IAS)** turned waste-pickers into "Green Commandos".

Ethical governance today requires not just knowledge of rules but **moral imagination, contextual intelligence and creative problem-solving**.
