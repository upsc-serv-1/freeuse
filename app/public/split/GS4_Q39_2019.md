**Three basic values and their importance
1. Integrity (Honesty and probity in conduct) - Acting truthfully, transparently and consistently with moral and legal standards.
1. **• **Importance-
1. Prevents corruption and misuse of public office.
1. Builds citizens’ trust in institutions.
1. Protects fairness in decision-making.
1. Strengthens legitimacy of the state.
1. **• **Illustration- Sanjiv Chaturvedi (IFS) exposed corruption in AIIMS despite facing harassment.
1. **Impartiality (Fairness and non-discrimination) - Treating all individuals and groups equally, without bias based on identity, status or political affiliation.
1. **Importance-
1. Upholds equality before law.
1. Prevents favouritism and discrimination.
1. Stability during Political Transitions
1. Impartial officer acts as a trusted mediator in conflict resolution
1. **Illustration- T.N. Seshan, as CEC, enforcing MCC in fair manner.
1. **Accountability - Being answerable for decisions and taking responsibility for consequences.
1. **• **Importance-
1. ** Feedback Loop- facilitates a two-way communication between state and citizens
1. Improves transparency and performance.
1. Enables democratic control over bureaucracy.
1. Promotes ethical and careful decision-making.
1. **•** Illustration - Ashwini Vaishnaw took moral responsibility after the Odisha train accident by personally overseeing relief and restoration.
These values ensure that **"Steel Frame" does not **rust or bend under the weight of corruption and bias.
