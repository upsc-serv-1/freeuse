## Question 13 (Year: 2020 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2020-gs4-q13-civilsdaily**

**2020 - “The current internet expansion has instilled a different set of cultural values which are often in conflict with traditional values.’ Discuss.** [Year: 2020] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

As per **ITU,** over **6 billion** people globally and over **86% of households in India** use the internet. This expansion of the internet has acted as a **double-edged sword.**

**Emergence of New Cultural Norms in Conflict with Traditional Norms**

1. **Individualism vs. Collectivism -** Eg- emergence of nuclear families and single parent parent families replacing joint families

2. **Instant Gratification vs. Patience (Tapas) -** Eg- rise in **"Speed-Dating" apps** and "Swipe Culture" conflicting with traditional marriage systems

3. **Egalitarianism vs. Seniority -** Youth questioning traditional family hierarchies or social structures based on information found online. **Eg-** rise in inter caste marriages

4. **Transparency vs. Discretion - Eg-** culture of "oversharing" personal lives (Eg- vlogging) conflicts with traditional value of "keeping family matters private"

5. **Global vs. Ethnic Identity -** Eg- dilution of local linguistic dialects and regional folk songs in favor of "standardized" global pop culture.

6. **Fluid vs. Fixed Identity -** fluid "career hopping" encouraged by the internet is seen as "instability" by traditionalists who value life-long commitment.

7. **Materialism vs. Contentment -** constant consumption and luxury displays in conflict with traditional values of simple living and **“santosh.”** Eg- "FOMO" (Fear of Missing Out) marketing.

8. **Digital Nomadism vs. Rootedness -** weakening of local community bonds due to high mobility among youth.

9. **Crowd Wisdom vs. Institutional Expertise - Eg-** diagnosing health issues via "Dr. Google" or Reddit threads instead of certified doctors

10. **Moral Relativism vs. Absolute Codes. Eg- rising** social tension regarding traditional views on gender roles, and diet.

### Counter-Argument- Internet expansion strengthening traditional values.

1. Internet-driven **"Wellness Culture"** has expanded reach of Yoga and Ayurveda

2. **Linguistic conservation**. Eg- **"People’s Linguistic Survey of India"** use digital recording to preserve tribal dialects like Santhali or Tulu

3. **Market Access for Traditional Artisans** - Eg- Platforms like **Etsy, Okhai, or GoCoop** allow Kalamkari artists to sell directly to global markets

4. **Live Streaming of temple Aartis (like Kashi Vishwanath)** allows virtual religious participation.

5. Food bloggers and YouTube channels focused on **"Village Cooking"** have documented rare regional culinary techniques. Eg- Village Food Factory

6. Modernizing Traditional Education. Eg- Vedic Math courses on Ed-tech

7. **Social Media as a Tool for "Cultural Assertion" - Eg-** Trends like **#SareeTwitter or #DhotiChallenge**

8. Emerging digital **"Sustainability" values** align with ancient traditional concepts of minimal living. Eg-**“slow fashion”** brands like **Ka Sha**

The emergence of **‘glocalization’** represents the fusion of internet driven modernity and community-oriented wisdom of tradition.
