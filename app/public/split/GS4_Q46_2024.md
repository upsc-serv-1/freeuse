## Question 46 (Year: 2024 | Paper: GS IV)

**2024 - Mission Karmayogi is aiming for maintaining a very high standard of conduct and behaviour to ensure efficiency for serving citizens and in turn developing oneself. How will this scheme empower civil servants in enhancing productive efficiency and delivering the services at the grassroots level? [Year: 2024] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

**Mission Karmayogi (National Programme for Civil Services Capacity Building) aims to transform the civil services from rule-based to role-based, competency-driven and citizen-centric administrative systems.
**Vision of civil servants
1. **1. Imaginative & Innovative
1. **2. Proactive & Polite
1. **3. Professional & Progressive
1. **4. Energetic & Enabling
1. **5. Transparent & Tech-enabled
1. **6. Creative & Constructive
### **Enhancing Productive Efficiency and Grassroots Service Delivery
1. Shift to Role-based Governance by mapping specific competencies to specific roles (FRAC) - ensures that the "right man is in the right job" .
1. iGOT-Karmayogi Platform- provides "anytime-anywhere" learning - allows a field officer in a remote district to access global best practices.
1. Democratization of Training- Karmayogi extends high-quality training to **Group B and C officials who are the actual frontline of grassroots delivery.
1. It moves away from "one-time" training at the start of a career to a **continuous learning life-cycle, keeping officials updated on evolving technologies and laws.
1. Dedicated modules on **"Soft Skills" empower officials to move from being "authorities" to "facilitators" - improves quality of citizen interaction at local offices.
1. Common training modules foster a **"Whole-of-Government" approach - **breaks departmental silos
1. Evidence-based Training- Using data analytics from the iGOT platform, the government can identify "competency gaps" in specific districts and deploy targeted training.
1. It creates a **uniform "Global Standard" of Indian civil service conduct, ensuring a citizen receives the same quality of service whether in Delhi or a tribal block.
1. Public-Private Partnership in Content- By curating content from global experts and private institutions, it brings **"Corporate Efficiency" into the public sector mindset.
1. Institutionalized Feedback- Eg- **360-degree feedback mechanism
1. Focus on Emotional Intelligence (EI) which is crucial for handling sensitive grassroots issues like land acquisition or communal harmony.
### **Potential Challenges
1. Digital Divide
1. Resistance to Change (deep-rooted "Babu Culture")
1. Language Barriers for frontline staff.
1. Weak incentives - Learning not strongly linked to promotions.
1. Infrastructure gaps - Lack of devices for frontline workers.
1. Over-standardisation ignores local realities.
Effectively implemented, it can help build **citizen centric and future ready civil service with right Attitude, Skills and Knowledge.
