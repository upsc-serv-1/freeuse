## Question 24 (Year: 2025 | Paper: GS IV)

**2025 - Carl von Clausewitz once said, “War is a diplomacy by other means.” Critically analyse the above statement in the present context of contemporary geo-political conflict. [Year: 2025] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

In his seminal work **On War, **Carl von Clausewitz suggests that war is not an end in itself but a rational instrument of statecraft used to achieve political goals when traditional diplomatic dialogue fails.
### **War is diplomacy by other means
1. Force used to extract **political concessions. Eg-Russia’s invasion of Ukraine to block NATO expansion.
1. War alters facts on ground to **strengthen bargaining position. Eg-Control of Crimea and Donbas.
1. Power signalling to deters rivals. Eg-China’s military exercises near Taiwan.
1. War reshapes **diplomatic alignments. Eg-Pakistan-China ‘all weather friendship’ after 1962 and 1965 wars
1. Resource diplomacy -Control over energy and minerals via force.
1. Negotiation through attrition -Prolonged fighting forces compromise. Eg-US withdrawl from Afghanistan
1. Policy of ‘bait and bloodletting’. Eg- US support to Ukraine to weaken Russia
1. Peace enforcement. Eg-NATO intervention in Kosovo.
1. War is used as a tool for **regime change. Eg- US intervention in Iraq
### **Counter-argument- War is not diplomacy but its failure
1. Humanitarian catastrophe violates **utilitarian principle of ‘greatest happiness’. Eg- over **1.4 million Russian deaths in Ukraine war
1. Rise of Non-State Actors- Groups like **ISIS-K dont pursue war for diplomatic aims but for ideological and religious aims.
1. War used to consolidate domestic **political legitimacy rather than national interest. Eg-Wartime
nationalism in Pakistan.
1. Due to ‘**complex interdependence’ war as a diplomatic means is not a **rational choice. Eg- US-China relations
1. War leads to **breakdown of diplomatic negotiations channels for peaceful resolution. Eg- Security Council paralysis.
1. While diplomacy is based on reason war is based on **passion.
1. The acute **security dilemma and zero-sum mentality fosters diplomatic distrust and reduce possibility of diplomatic cooperation. Eg- India’s suspension of Indus water treaty.
As per Deontological ethics, means of war cant justify any ends. As pointed out by **Bertrand Russell, "War does not determine who is right - only who is left."
