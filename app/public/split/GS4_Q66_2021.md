## Question 66 (Year: 2021 | Paper: GS IV)

**2021 - Impact of digital technology as a reliable source of input for rational decision making is a debatable issue. Critically evaluate with suitable examples. [Year: 2021] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

“Technology is a useful servant but a dangerous master.” - **Christian Lous Lange
Digital technology provides vast data infrastructure for modern governance, yet its reliability as an input for objective, rational decision-making remains highly controversial.
### **Digital Technology as a Reliable Source of Input for Rational Decision-Making
1. Real-Time Policy Inputs: Eg: CoWIN platform enabled real-time vaccine inventory planning across all Indian districts.
1. Citizen Participation in Policy- Eg- MyGov collected over 10 crore citizen suggestions that shaped the National Education Policy 2020.
1. Digitizing demographic and macro-surveys significantly eliminates human enumeration mistakes and calculation errors.
1. Satellite tracking and geospatial maps provide precise objective inputs for infrastructure projects. Eg: PM GatiShakti National Master Plan GIS data.
1. Leakage Elimination- Digital systems make welfare delivery accurate and eliminate corruption effectively. Eg- DBT transferred over ₹44 lakh crore and saved ₹3.48 lakh crore
1. Interlinked digital architectures **unify isolated departmental databases into a singular, holistic policy-making dashboard. Helps eliminate duplication.
1. Predictive Weather Planning: Eg: The IMD's advanced Doppler radar systems supplying precise cyclone trajectory inputs to save coastal communities.
### **Counter-Argument: The Flaws and Risks of Digital Inputs
1. Poor data collection practices hampers objective policy making and implementation. Eg- Ghost Beneficiaries under Ayushman Bharat.
1. The "Black Box" Problem- AI logic is often opaque.
1. Institutionalizing Historical Bias- If data is biased , the AI will "learn" and automate that bias. Eg- **US COMPAS tool biased against African-Americans.
1. Difficulty in ensuring accountability for mistakes.
1. Correlation vs Causation Fallacies: Analytical engines can link two completely unrelated data trends together, generating irrational choices.
1. Exclusion of the Digitally Illiterate from policy making & online grievance portals
### **Way Forward
1. Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)- The final "sign-off," especially in cases affecting human rights, must be by a human officer.
1. Mandating **regular 3rd-party audits of government algorithms to detect and "unlearn" biases.
1. The EU AI Act Approach- "Risk-Based Framework" where high-risk AI (policing or judiciary) face the highest level of ethical regulation.
1. Digital Ethics Commissions including ethicists, jurists, and technologists to oversee AI deployment in public service.
1. Ethical Coding Standards- Teaching **"Ethics by Design" to programmers working on public infrastructure.
While digital technology streamlines administrative efficiency, it cannot replace human empathy, requiring a balanced model where data informs but conscience rules.
