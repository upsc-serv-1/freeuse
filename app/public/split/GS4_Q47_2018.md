## Question 47 (Year: 2018 | Paper: GS IV)

**2018 - What is meant by conflict of interest? Illustrate with examples, the difference between the actual and potential conflicts of interest. [Year: 2018] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

In public administration, a **Conflict of Interest (COI) is a situation where the **private interests of a public official clash with their **professional duties and responsibilities.
**Manifestation of conflict of interest
1. Financial Investments in companies regulated by one's own department.
1. Family Ties (Nepotism). Eg- Chanda Kochhar Case
1. Post-Employment Ambitions (Revolving Door). Eg- "Amakudari" in Japan
1. Gifts and Hospitality
1. Outside Directorships
1. Personal Biases
1. Secondary Employment. Eg- Moonlighting
8. **Property Interests
**Actual vs. Potential Conflict of Interest
**Feature
**Actual Conflict of Interest
**Potential Conflict of Interest
**Definition
A direct and current clash between duty and interest.
A situation where a conflict might arise in the future.
**Nature
Real and existing
Foreseeable
**Legal Standing
Usually a violation of conduct rules if not disclosed.
Not a violation yet, but requires a "management plan."
**Ethical
**Standing
Violation of duty and probity
Ethical risk requiring disclosure
**Public
**Perception
Seen as active corruption or bias.
Seen as a risk factor that needs monitoring.
**Action Needed
**Recusal (stepping down) or Divestment.
**Disclosure (declaring the interest).
**Result
Immediate compromise of objectivity.
Future risk of compromised objectivity.
**Example
awarding a contract to a company owned by his spouse.
framing mining policy while owning mining stocks
### **Ways to Prevent Conflict of Interest
1. Mandatory Disclosure of Assets (**2nd ARC)
1. Cooling-off Period
1. Code of Ethics and office of Ethics Commissioner
1. Institutionalized "Conflict of Interest" Registers
1. Integrity Pacts
In a democracy, the **appearance of integrity is as important as integrity itself. Moving from a **culture of secrecy to a culture of disclosure, can help maintain public trust in governance.
