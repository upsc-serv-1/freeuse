## Question 25 (Year: 2020 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2020-gs4-q25-civilsdaily**

**2020 - “Hatred is destructive of a person’s wisdom and conscience that can poison a nation’s spirit.’ Do you agree with this view? Justify your answer.** [Year: 2020] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

"Holding on to anger is like grasping a hot coal with the intent of throwing it at someone else; you are the one who gets burned." - **The Buddha**

**Hatred** is a deep-seated negative emotion marked by hostility, prejudice and desire to harm, which clouds moral judgement and rational thought.

### Hatred destroying a person’s wisdom and conscience

1. **Corruption of character (Virtue Ethics)** - prejudice and anger replace virtues like patience and tolerance. Eg- German society under Hitler

2. **Erosion of practical wisdom** and balanced judgement. Eg- Mob violence due to rumours on WhatsApp

3. **Loss of empathy** and compassion. Eg- In the **Rwandan Genocide (1994)**, Tutsis dehumanised as "cockroaches"

4. **Cognitive bias amplification** - Hatred confirms prejudice, not truth. Eg- Conspiracy theories about communities.

5. Stoics like **Marcus Aurelius** argued that hatred is a **"sickness of the soul."** Eg- torture of PoW or Concentration camps

6. **Vindictiveness over Justice-** A wise person seeks justice, while a hateful person seeks revenge. Eg-**Treaty of Versailles (1919)** was driven by French desire for "revenge"

7. **Violation of Kantian duty** - Treating others as means, not ends. Eg- "My Lai Massacre" during Vietnam war.

8. **Instrumentalisation of reason** - Intelligence used to harm, not to heal. Eg- doxxing

9. **Psychological degradation** - Hatred increases anxiety, anger, and stress. Eg- Radicalised individuals like Ajmal Kasab.

10. **Stagnation of Personal Growth-** Hateful person focuses on past grievances rather than on future or finding peace.

### Hatred Poisoning a Nation’s Spirit

1. **Erosion of Social Capital-** A nation’s spirit relies on "Fraternity." Hatred replaces cooperation with suspicion. Eg- communal riots during elections

2. **Institutional Decay-** When hatred enters the national spirit, institutions also get weaponized. Eg-

3. **The Normalization of Violence-** When hatred is mainstream, violence is seen as a "cleansing" act. Eg-ethnic cleansing of Jews in Nazi Germany

4. **Weakening of democratic culture**. Eg- Labelling critics as anti-national.

5. **Fragmentation of national identity**. Eg- Religion-based or ethnicity-based politics.

6. **The Post-Truth Nation-** propaganda takes over objective truth due to ‘superflous masses’.

7. **Global reputational damage.** Eg- **Apartheid regime in South Africa**

8. **The Cycle of Reciprocity-** Hatred in one group triggers hatred in another, creating a "Death Spiral." Eg- Ethnic conflict in Manipur

9. **Stifling of Creativity and Innovation** as hateful spirit is a closed spirit. Eg- **"Brain Drain" from 1930s Germany**.

10. **Intergenerational Trauma-** Hatred is a legacy that ensures the spirit of the nation remains poisoned for decades. **Eg- Partition of India (1947)**

"Darkness cannot drive out darkness; only light can do that. Hate cannot drive out hate; only love can do that." - **Martin Luther King Jr.**
