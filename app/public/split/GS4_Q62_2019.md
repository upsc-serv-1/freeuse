## Question 62 (Year: 2019 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2019-gs4-q62-civilsdaily**

**2019 - Explain the basic principles of the citizens charter movement and bring out its importance.** [Year: 2019] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

The **Citizen’s Charter**, introduced in India in **1997 (DARPG)** following the **UK model**, aims to make public services **transparent, accountable, and citizen-centric** by clearly defining service standards, timelines, and grievance redressal mechanisms.

### Basic Principles of the Citizens' Charter Movement

1. **Standards:** Explicitly state the **quality and level of service** citizens can expect, ideally with **specific, measurable timeframes.**

2. **Quality:** Commit to **continuously improving the standard** of services provided to meet user expectations.

3. **Choice:** Offer citizens a variety of **choices and options when accessing services,** wherever feasible.

4. **Transparency:** Ensure **openness in rules, procedures, schemes,** and grievance redress mechanisms.

5. **Accountability:** Hold individual officials and organizations **answerable for the services delivered** and commitments made.

6. **Value:** Ensure that services provide good value **for taxpayers' money.**

### Importance of citizen charters

1. **Defines Service Standards - Eg-** Passport Seva Kendra specifies delivery within **3 working days** for Tatkal applications.

2. **Enhances Administrative Transparency -** Makes **procedures, responsibilities, and timelines public**, reducing scope for arbitrariness and discretion.

3. **Promotes Accountability of Officials -** Identifies **responsible officers** for each service and grievance redressal, ensuring **answerability for delays or failures**.

4. **Empowers Citizens** to **demand better service delivery**, question inefficiencies, and seek **grievance redressal** through defined channels.

5. Builds **mutual expectations** between government and citizens, enhancing trust in public institutions.

6. Provides a **benchmark for assessing departmental efficiency** and monitoring service outcomes through periodic audits.

7. Promotes feedback-based improvement by institutionalizing citizen input in service reforms.

**Case Studies**

1. **Indian Railways:** compensation, cleanliness standards, and punctuality targets.

2. **Taxpayers’ Charter:** Defines rights like privacy and fair treatment.

By aligning it with the **2nd ARC recommendations**, it can evolve from a symbolic commitment to a **practical framework for responsive, transparent, and citizen-centric governance**.
