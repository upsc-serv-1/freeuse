## Question 11 (Year: 2020 | Paper: GS IV)

**2020 - Discuss the role of ethics and values in enhancing the following three major components of Comprehensive National Power (CNP) viz. human capital, soft power (culture and policies) and social harmony. [Year: 2020] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

CNP is a **holistic measure of a **nation's ability to pursue its strategic objectives. It includes
1. **1. "Hard" dimensions - military, economy, territory
1. **2. "Soft" dimensions - culture, governance, social cohesion.
**Role of ethics and values in enhancing CNP
**Human Capital
1. Integrity in the education system prevents systemic corruption like paper leaks or degree fraud.
1. Values like **punctuality and **"Dharma" increase **productivity. Eg- Japanese concept of **"Kaizen"
1. Ethics promotes **meritocracy by ensuring fairness in recruitment - prevents **"Brain Drain"
1. Creating a **healthy and resilient population - Values ensure that medical professionals **prioritize patient care over commercial gain.
1. Values of **gender equality and respect unlock 50% of the nation’s latent human capital.
1. The trusteeship model encourages "Giving Back" through CSR or philanthropy. Eg- **Tata Group
### **Soft Power
1. Cultural diplomacy - Yoga and Ayurveda, rooted in the value of holistic well-being, have become India's most successful soft-power exports.
1. Values of **tolerance and pluralism projects **diversity as strength.
1. Ethics provides moral authority in foreign policy- Eg- India’s **"Vaccine Maitri" based on philosophy of "Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam".
1. Values of **peaceful co-existence make a nation a **"benign power." Eg- India’s history of **Non-Alignment and "No First Use" nuclear policy.
1. India’s lead in the **International Solar Alliance reflects a moral commitment to the planet (Prakriti) and **environmental stewardship.
1. Humanitarian Assistance- Eg- **Operation SamudraBandhu (Sri Lanka Cyclones) showcased India as a "First Responder".
1. Democratic values enhance global image. Eg- Free and fair elections in India.
### **Social Harmony
1. Secularism and Tolerance prevents communal conflicts. Eg- **Syncretic "Ganga-Jamuni Tehzeeb"
1. A sense of **fraternity (Article 51A) ensures that citizens support each other during crises.
1. When the marginalized feel the system is **"just and fair," they are less likely to lean toward radicalism or insurgency. Eg- hearts and minds strategy in J&K
1. Values of **"Antyodaya" ensure that economic growth is inclusive. Eg- PM KISAN Scheme
1. High **mutual trust among citizens builds **social capital.
1. Conflict Resolution through Dialogue- Eg- **peaceful resolution of the Ram Janmabhoomi issue
**Challenges
1. Erosion of Integrity- Eg- politician-bureaucrat-corporate nexus (Vohra committee)
1. Hyper-Nationalism vs. Global Values
1. Digital Disruption- Eg- "Fake News" and hate speech
1. Inequality- 1% owning 41% wealth (Oxfam)
1. Crisis of Character- "Banalization of Evil"
By harmonizing its ancient wisdom (Ahimsa, Satya, Asteya) with modern constitutional values (Justice, Liberty, Equality), India can become a **Vishwaguru
