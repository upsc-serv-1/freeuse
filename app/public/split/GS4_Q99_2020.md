## Question 99 (Year: 2020 | Paper: GS IV)

**2020 - What teachings of Buddha are most relevant today and why? Discuss. [Year: 2020] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

Gautama Buddha’s ancient teachings offer a highly practical, rational psychological manual to navigate modern global crises, ethical dilemmas, and mental health challenges.
### **Major teachings of Buddha and their relevance in the contemporary world
### Four Noble Truths - Understanding suffering and its causes.
1. a. Encourages self-reflection and emotional resilience in modern life challenges.
1. b. Relevant in addressing rising mental health disorders and social dissatisfaction.
1. **2. Middle Path - Balanced life avoiding extreme luxury and severe austerity.
1. a. Helps reduce **consumerism, stress, and burnout in modern competitive societies. Eg-Corporate mindfulness programmes
1. b. Encourages sustainable and disciplined lifestyles amid rising materialism and mental pressure. Eg: India’s Mission LiFE
1. Ahimsa (Non-violence) - Non-injury in thought, word, and action.
1. a. Promotes peace amid **war, terrorism, and communal violence. Eg- Russia-Ukraine conflict
1. b. Inspired global non-violent movements and peace-building initiatives. Eg- Gandhian freedom struggle
1. Karuna (Compassion) - Empathy and concern for suffering humanity.
1. a. Encourages humanitarian support during disasters, pandemics, and refugee crises. Eg- India’s Vaccine Maitri initiative
1. b. Strengthens inclusive welfare and social justice policies in democratic governance.
1. Eightfold Path - Ethical conduct, right livelihood, and disciplined behaviour.
1. a. Promotes **ethical governance, integrity, and responsible citizenship.
1. b. Helps combat corruption, greed, and unethical professional practices.
1. Equality and Anti-Caste Outlook - Rejection of caste hierarchy and discrimination.
1. a. Supports social equality and dignity for marginalised communities. Eg- Dalit empowerment movements
1. b. Strengthens constitutional ideals of **justice, equality, and fraternity.
1. Rational Inquiry - Encouraged questioning over blind faith and superstition.
1. a. Promotes scientific temper and evidence-based thinking in modern societies.
1. b. Helps combat **fake news, superstition, and misinformation in digital age.
1. Universal Brotherhood - Respect and harmony among all human beings.
1. a. Promotes tolerance and peaceful coexistence in multicultural societies.
1. b. Supports ideals of **global peace and Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam.
1. **9. Anicca (Impermanence) - Understanding that all earthly conditions, joy, and sorrow are in a state of constant change.
1. a. Builds internal psychological resilience during rapid global transitions and economic disruptions.
1. b. Discourages arrogance of power and material hoarding, fostering humility in public life.
In an age of rapid technological, economic, and climate change, his message of **ethical self-transformation and harmony with all life forms offers a timeless path for building a peaceful and sustainable world.
