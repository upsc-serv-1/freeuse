## Question 28 (Year: 2022 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2022-gs4-q28-civilsdaily**

**2022 - The Rules and Regulations provided to all the civil servants are same, yet there is difference in the performance. Positive minded officers are able to interpret the Rules and Regulations in favour of the case and achieve success, whereas negative minded officers are unable to achieve goals by interpreting the same Rules and Regulations against the case. Discuss with illustrations.** [Year: 2022] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

In the realm of public administration, the rulebook provides the **"Letter of the Law,"** but the officer's mindset provides the **"Spirit of the Law."**

**Alternative introduction**

Rules and regulations are meant to ensure **uniformity, legality and accountability** in

administration. However, their application depends on the **attitude, values and interpretation** of the officers.

### Positive-Minded Officers- Interpreting Rules for Success

1. **Invoking "Residual Powers" for Innovation.** Eg- IAS **Prashant Nair** used crowdfunding for community kitchens under **Project Compassion** in Kozhikode.

2. **Flexible during Emergencies.** Eg- procuring cylinders via "Spot Purchase" clause during COVID 19

3. **Promoting "Ease of Doing Business".** Eg- Interpreting licensing norms to encourage entrepreneurship.

4. **Utilizing "Spirit of the Law"** to uphold **justice** Eg- Allowing free treatment under government hospitals for undocumented homeless.

5. Adherence to **utilitarian** principle of **“greatest happiness”** by creative rule interpretation. **Eg-** using MNREGA funds to build school playgrounds

6. **Environmental Stewardship** using the "Precautionary Principle." Eg- Halting a project near a wetland by interpreting "Ecological Sensitivity" broadly

7. **Humanizing Service Rules-** Dealing with subordinates with empathy. **Eg-** Granting "Child Care Leave" to a single father

8. **Inclusive urban planning**. Eg- Designating specific "Vending Zones" for street hawkers

9. **Active Conflict Resolution- Eg-** Using "Section 144" to create a "Protected Space" for a peaceful dialogue between administration and aggrieved public.

### Negative-Minded Officers- Interpreting Rules Against the Case

1. Suffers from **"Bureaucratic Apathy"** or **"Status Quo Bias."** Eg- resistance to "E-Office" by citing vague "Data Privacy" concerns.

2. **Red Tapism-** Eg- denying **ration** under PDS due to biometric error (Jharkhand case)

3. **"Passing the Buck"-** Interpreting jurisdiction rules to avoid taking responsibility. Eg- delay in medical aid in inter-state border accidents

4. Avoid **transparency and accountability.** Eg- invoking "National Security" clause to deny RTI

5. **Strict "Hierarchy" interpretation- Example-** Refusing to meet a citizen delegation because they did not have a "Prior Appointment".

6. **Focus on Punitive Action-** Interpreting discipline rules solely for punishment.

7. **Obstructive Auditing-** Using "Post-Facto" criticism to stall future initiatives.

8. **Resource Hoarding-** Refusing to spend funds to avoid "Audit Objections." Eg- consisting lapsing of **Tribe Sub-plan funds**

There is need to shift from a **"Command and Control" mindset** to **"Collaborate and Care" attitude** for transforming civil services from “rusted” steel frame to **“trusted steel frame”**
