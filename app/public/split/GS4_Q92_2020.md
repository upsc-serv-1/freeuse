## Question 92 (Year: 2020 | Paper: GS IV)

**2020 - “The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others.” - Mahatma Gandhi [Year: 2020] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

Gandhi suggests that **true self-realisation does not come from selfish pursuit of pleasure, power or status, but from selfless service to others.
### **Importance of selfless service for self-realisation
1. Reduces ego (self-centeredness)- Serving others shifts focus from self to society. Eg- Volunteers during disasters working anonymously.
1. Builds empathy - Understanding others’ suffering deepens compassion. Eg- **Doctors without Borders (MSF) professionals working in conflict zones
1. Gives purpose (Ikigai)- Life gains meaning beyond material success. Eg- **Narayan Krishnan, a high-profile chef, gave up his career to feed the homeless
1. Emotional Intelligence- Constant interaction with diverse people in service settings refines our ability to manage emotions and understand human nature.
1. Overcoming Fear- Eg- **Freedom fighters like Bhagat Singh lost their fear of imprisonment because their "self" was entirely merged with the service of their nation's freedom.
1. Develops character - Service nurtures humility and sense of responsibility. Eg- Mother Teresa’s lifelong care for the destitute.
1. Creates inner peace - Altruism reduces anxiety and emptiness. Eg- **Baba Amte, by serving leprosy
patients
1. Promotes ethical clarity - Service aligns actions with values. Eg- Honest civil servants resisting corruption.
1. Enhances leadership - Servant leaders earn trust. Eg- Mahatma Gandhi’s work for Harijan.
1. Building Resilience- Handling the challenges of others builds a mental "callus" that helps us face our own life's adversities with equanimity.
1. Universal Connection- Service leads to the realization of Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam (The World is One Family), where the "Self" is no longer an island but part of the whole.
### **Ethical framework for selfless service
1. ** Karma Yoga - Action without attachment to reward.
1. ** Virtue ethics - Cultivation of compassion, humility, generosity.
1. ** Ethics of care - Moral priority to relationships and vulnerability.
1. ** Utilitarianism - Maximising well-being of others.
1. ** Gandhian Sarvodaya (welfare of all).
1. ** Purity of Motive (Intention)
We discover our true selves not through possession, but through contribution. Service refines the soul, strengthens society and gives life enduring meaning.
“Service to mankind is service to god” - **Swami Vivekanand
