## Question 70 (Year: 2018 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2018-gs4-q70-civilsdaily**

**2018 - Distinguish between “Code of ethics” and “Code of conduct” with suitable examples.** [Year: 2018] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

A **Code of Ethics **is a set of moral values guiding behaviour, while a **Code of Conduct **prescribes specific rules regulating actions.

**Difference between code of conduct and code of ethics**

| **Code of Ethics** | **Code of Conduct** |

| --- | --- |

| Moral principles guiding decisions. Eg- **Judicial integrity in Supreme Court judges** | Behavioural rules regulating actions. Eg- **CCS Conduct Rules, 1964** |

| Value-oriented framework. Eg- **Medical ethics of patient confidentiality** | Rule-oriented framework. Eg- **Mandatory asset declaration by IAS officers** |

| Broad ethical ideals. Eg- **Police impartiality during communal violence** | Specific operational norms. Eg- **Election duty neutrality guidelines** |

| Encourages conscience-based actions. Eg-
**Whistleblowing against corruption** | Requires formal compliance. Eg- **Ban on accepting government gifts** |

| Flexible ethical guidance. Eg- **Compassion during disaster relief operations** | Rigid administrative instructions. Eg- **Official social media usage restrictions** |

| Self-regulatory principles. Eg- **Journalistic commitment to truthfulness** | Institutionally enforceable rules. Eg-
**Suspension for workplace harassment** |

| Focuses on “right conduct”. Eg- **Fairness in judicial verdicts** | Focuses on “allowed conduct”. Eg-
**Attendance and punctuality requirements** |

| Moral violation harms credibility. Eg- **Biased reporting by journalists** | Rule violation attracts penalties. Eg-**Dismissal for bribery charges** |

| Shapes ethical culture. Eg- **Corporate transparency and accountability values** | Maintains organisational discipline. Eg-
**Uniform code in armed forces** |

| Inspired by ideals and philosophy. Eg- **Gandhian principles in public service** | Derived from legal regulations. Eg- **Service rules for civil servants** |

### Significance of “Code of Ethics” and “Code of Conduct”

**1. Promote Integrity & Accountability**

**2. Strengthen Public Trust**

**3. Guide Ethical Decision Making**

**4. Prevent Corruption**

**5. Improve Professional Discipline**

**6. Support Good Governance**

Together, **Code of Ethics **and **Code of Conduct **create morally responsible, disciplined, and accountable institutions essential for ethical governance.
