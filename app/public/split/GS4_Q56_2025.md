**2025 - “For any kind of social re-engineering by successfully implementing welfare schemes, a civil servant must use reason and critical thinking in an ethical framework.” Justify this statement with suitable examples.** [Year: 2025] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

Social re-engineering refers to the deliberate attempt to **transform social structures, behaviors, and cultural norms **to achieve a more **equitable society. Civil servants act as a **“facilitator” **of this change.

### Need for reason and critical thinking in an ethical framework for successful social engineering

1.** Diagnose real problems, not symptoms **- Eg- Nutrition deficits need diet diversity, not only calories.

3. Uphold **constitutional morality over social morality. **Eg-**conditional cash transfer **under **"Kanyashree" **scheme improved efficiency.

4.** Design context-specific solutions. Eg- Jal Jeevan Mission adapted to local water sources.

5.** Avoid one-size-fits-all policies. Eg- location specific housing designs under PM Awas.

6.** Prevent unintended harm. Eg- advising against loan waivers to avoid moral hazard.

7.** Balancing competing values. Eg- balancing efficiency and privacy in Aadhaar.

8.** Ensure bottoms up planning. Eg- fair, prior and informed consent of Gram Sabha under PESA.

9.** Prevent elite capture **of policy. Eg- Social audits in MGNREGA.

10.** Promote behavioural change, not just compliance. Eg- Gandhigiri in SBM - giving flowers for open defecation.

11.** Ensure sustainability **(intergenerational justice). Eg- **PM-PRANAM **to promote organic farming

12.** Protect rights while delivering welfare. Eg- Consent-based data use in health IDs.

Social transformation requires **critical reasoning to design effective interventions and ethical grounding to ensure justice, dignity and inclusion.
