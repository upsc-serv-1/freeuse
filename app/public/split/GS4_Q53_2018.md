## Question 53 (Year: 2018 | Paper: GS IV)

**2018 - “In doing a good thing, everything is permitted which is not prohibited expressly or by clear implication”. Examine the statement with suitable examples in the context of a public servant discharging his/her duties. [Year: 2018] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

This statement reflects the principle of **Administrative Discretion and **Innovation in public service. It also highlights the shift from **"Rule-based" to **"Goal-oriented" governance.
**Everything is permitted in doing good thing
1. Adherence to the utilitarian **principle of ‘greatest happiness.’
1. Resource optimization- Eg- **Armstrong Pame (IAS) crowdfunded the "People’s Road".
1. Innovation for reformative justice- Kiran Bedi (IPS) introduced Vipassana.
1. Behavioral Nudging (Gandhigiri)- Using psychology instead of force. Eg- In the **Swachh Bharat Abhiyan, officers gifted flowers to people defecating in the open.
1. Prioritising **social welfare by bypassing bureaucratic rigidity. Eg- **Prashant Nair’s "Operation Sulaimani" - Use of crowdsourced coupons to feed hunger
1. Finding **‘out of the box’ solutions. Eg- IPS **chetan singh Rathore using National Anthem to manage protesting crowd.
1. Ethical use of discretion in crises. Eg- **Dr. Syed Sehrish Asgar used her office as a "human call center" during communication blackouts in J&K
1. Helps in **collaborative problem solving rather than a top-down approach. Eg- S.R. Sankaran involved local community elders in identifying bonded laborers.
1. Creativity and innovations facilitate **adoption of technology to solve problems. Eg- **Dr. Hari Chandana building pavements from recycled plastic in Hyderabad.
1. Inaction out of fear of rules is often a greater "evil" than **Innovation within the framework of the law. -leads to **policy paralysis
### **Boundaries that Need to be Followed
1. Constitutional Morality- One cannot "do good" by violating
Fundamental Rights. Eg- unauthorized phone tapping.
1. Financial Propriety- Eg- following **due process in tender allocation
1. Avoiding Conflict of Interest through declaration and recusal
1. Acting in the **"spirit" of the law
1. Dignity and Human Rights- Adherence to Kant’s categorical imperative.
1. Non-arbitrariness - No favouritism under the garb of welfare.
The ultimate test for any "permitted" action is **Mahatma Gandhi's Talisman- "Will it lead to the upliftment of the poorest?"
### **Counter-Argument- The Risks of Unchecked Discretion

1. Erosion of Procedural Due Process- Eg- 2G spectrum case
2. Financial Irregularity and Corruption
3. "Rule of Men" over "Rule of Law." Eg- Bulldozer justice
4. Violation of Fundamental Rights- Eg- custodial torture
5. Policy Instability and Lack of Continuity
6. Risk of Political Misuse
1. **7. Moral Hazard of **normalising shortcuts. Eg- Illegal tapping to catch criminals.
1. Accountability Gap may lead to institutional Decay
1. The "Savior Complex"- can lead to administrative arrogance.
