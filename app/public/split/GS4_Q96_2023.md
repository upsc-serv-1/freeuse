## Question 96 (Year: 2023 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2023-gs4-q96-civilsdaily**

**2023 - Do not hate anybody, because that hatred that comes out from you must, in the long run, come back to you. If you love, that love will come back to you, completing the circle.” -Swami Vivekanand.** [Year: 2023] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

Vivekananda reminds us that human relationships operate in **moral feedback loops**- what we give to the world, the world gives back to us.

### Key Values

1.** Reciprocity**

2.** Non-Maleficence**

3.** Universal Brotherhood**

4.** Compassion**

5.** Equanimity**

### The Burden of Hatred

1.** Internal Emotional Corrosion: **Hatred destroys the mental peace and ethical clarity of the sender before harming anyone else.

2.** Breakdown of Trust: **Eg: A manager ruling by fear and suspicion triggers a toxic workplace culture of backstabbing.

3.** Certainty of Retaliation: **Aggressive emotional outputs set off a chain reaction of hostility that eventually cycles back.

4.** Loss of Moral Authority: **Exercising malice degrades an individual’s reputation and strips away their institutional power to inspire.

5.** Intergenerational Social Fractures: **Systemic hatred passed down to youth traps entire generations in endless cycles of violence. Eg- Pakistan’s hatred for India

### The Circle of Love

1.** Cultivation of Character: **Projecting compassion uplifts the individual's mind, creating a calm and balanced mental state. Eg: Nelson Mandela

2.** Fostering Civic Solidarity: **Offering selfless help strengthens community bonds, turning strangers into reliable social networks. Eg- free community kitchens (Langar)

3.** Reciprocal Institutional Goodwill: **Acts of genuine care build a reservoir of public trust that aids governance. Eg: Armstrong Pame building a 100km road in Manipur through crowd-funding.

4.** Promotion of Global Peace: **Eg: India's timely supply of life-saving medicines to smaller nations building lasting global goodwill.

5.** Generational Moral Continuity: **Eg: Baba Amte’s selfless tribal healthcare legacy inspiring his children to continue the work. (Dr. Prakash Amte)

### Relevance in Contemporary Governance

1.** Empathy-Led Public Service: **Treating vulnerable citizens with deep respect significantly minimizes public grievances and structural alienation.

2.** Restorative Criminal Justice: **Shifting focus from harsh punishment to social rehabilitation lowers repeat offense rates among criminals. Eg: Open jail systems

3.** Compassionate Welfare Administration: **Designing schemes with proactive empathy ensures that marginalized groups receive dignified institutional support.

4.** Collaborative Environmental Stewardship: **Treating nature with care rather than greed ensures resource security for our own survival.

5.** Inclusive Democratic Dialogue: **Engaging political opponents with dignity prevents polarization and preserves healthy, stable parliamentary governance.

By rejecting hatred and embracing universal empathy, public administrators and individuals alike can build a harmonious society where goodwill returns to complete a circle of collective progress.
