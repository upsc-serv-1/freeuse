## Question 63 (Year: 2021 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2021-gs4-q63-civilsdaily**

**2021 - An independent and empowered social audit mechanism is an absolute must in every sphere of public service, including judiciary, to ensure performance, accountability and ethical conduct. Elaborate.** [Year: 2021] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

A social audit is a democratic process where citizens directly review, evaluate, and verify the official records, expenditures, and actual field performance of public service institutions.

### Importance to Improve Performance of Public Service

1.** Optimizes Resource Utilization: **Eg: social audits under **MGNREGA **reduced leakages and improved rural asset quality in Andhra Pradesh

2.** Enhances Service Delivery Timelines: **Pressure from community monitoring forces officials to complete projects without bureaucratic delays.

3.** Improves Project Target Accuracy: **Eg: Verification drives under the **National Food Security Act **removed thousands of fake ration cards.

4.** Boosts Quality Standards: **Eg: Public scrutiny of rural school buildings and mid-day meals improves safety.

5.** Bridges Local Governance Gaps: **Community audits identify specific, missing civic amenities that top-down government planning often misses.

6.** Improves Judicial Case Management: **Citizen reviews of court functioning encourage registries to clear administrative delays and bottlenecks.

### Importance to Improve Accountability of Public Service

1.** Dismantles Bureaucratic Financial Opacity: **Eg: **Mazdoor Kisan Shakti Sangathan (MKSS) **pioneered public hearings to expose hidden corruption.

2.** Enforces Strict Consequence Mechanisms: **Clear evidence gathered during community audits forces authorities to penalize errant, corrupt officers.

3.** Checks Judicial Pendency Patterns: **Reviewing administrative timelines makes institutional court registries strictly accountable for systemic listing delays.

4.** Restricts Executive Fund Diversions: **Eg: Strict auditing of specialized **Scheduled Caste Sub-Plan **funds prevent diversion to general projects.

5.** Empowers Marginalized Civic Groups: **Social audits give institutional platforms to poor citizens to question powerful government decision-makers.

6.** Validates Right to Information: **Social audits turn passive information requests into an active, collective tool for community justice.

7.** Reduces Public Service Grievance Backlogs: **Institutionalizing community verification forces departments to quickly resolve long-pending public utility complaints.

### Importance to Improve Ethical Conduct

1.** Institutionalizes Public Service Integrity: **Regular public scrutiny conditions state officials to perform duties with high honesty and fairness.

2.** Discourages Conflict of Interest: **Public disclosure of project details prevents officials from giving lucrative contracts to relatives.

3.** Maintains High Judicial Ethics: **Reviewing court administration standards encourages judges to strictly follow codes of ethical conduct.

4.** Protects Citizen Dignity Rights: **Equal opportunity during public hearings prevents officials from displaying discriminatory caste or gender biases.

5.** Promotes Empathy in Administration: **Direct interactions with poor citizens during audits sensitive bureaucrats toward urgent field realities.

6.** Fosters Values of Truthfulness: **Eg: Stopping fraudulent documentation of ghost beneficiaries. Implementing independent social audits across all public spheres, including the judiciary, is vital to achieving absolute transparency, institutional integrity, and democratic governance.
