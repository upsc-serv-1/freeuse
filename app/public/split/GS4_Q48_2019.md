## Question 48 (Year: 2019 | Paper: GS IV)

**2019 - What do you understand by probity in governance? Based on your understanding of the term, suggest measures for ensuring probity in government. [Year: 2019] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

**Probity is a multifaceted concept representing the quality of having **strong moral principles, absolute integrity, and uprightness in the discharge of public duties.
### **Key Components of Probity
1. Integrity- Adherence to a strict moral and ethical code. E- Lal Bahadur Shastri
1. Transparency- Decisions and actions must be open to public scrutiny.
1. Accountability- Public servants must be answerable for their actions and omissions.
1. Objectivity- Decisions should be based on merit and evidence, free from bias or personal interest.
1. Impartiality- Acting without prejudice toward any particular group or individual.
1. Avoiding conflict of interest through disclosures and recusals
1. Frugality- Treating public money with more care than one's own
Ensuring probity requires a **"triple-lock" approach- **Structural, **Procedural, and **Behavioral.
1. **1. Institutional and Legal Reforms
1. Ensuring the independence and resource-sufficiency of bodies like **Lokpal, Lokayukta and **CVC
1. Rigorous implementation of the **Whistleblowers Protection Act.
1. Empowering the **CAG to conduct social audits and performance audits
1. ** Adoption of the "Hong Kong Model" (ICAC) to tackle corruption - three-pronged strategy of Investigation, Prevention, and Community Education
1. **2. Procedural and Technological Measures
1. ** E-Governance to reduce human discretion and the "gatekeeper" effect. Eg- **GeM
1. ** Citizens’ Charters- Mandating clear timelines for service delivery. Eg- SAKALA (Karnataka Guarantee of Services Act)
1. Proactive disclosure under **Section 4 of the RTI Act for transparency
1. Mandatory and periodic declaration of **assets and liabilities by civil servants
1. **3. Human Resource and Ethical Reforms
1. Moving beyond a "Rule-based" conduct code (what not to do) to a **"Value-based" code of ethics (what to strive for) (**2nd ARC).
1. ** Competency-linked training under **Mission Karmayogi to ensure civil servants are not only skilled but also ethically grounded.
1. **4. Social and Political Measures
1. ** Legal backing to Social Audits. (Meghalaya Act)
1. Ensuring **transparency in electoral funding to prevent the "quid-pro-quo" culture at top.
As pointed out by **PM Modi in Independence Day speech, promoting **‘suchita, pardarshita and nishpakshata’ (probity, transparency and impartiality) is essential for ‘Amrit Kaal’
