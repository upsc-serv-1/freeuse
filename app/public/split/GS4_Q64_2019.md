## Question 64 (Year: 2019 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2019-gs4-q64-civilsdaily**

**2019 - What is meant by ‘crisis of conscience’? How does it manifest itself in the public domain?** [Year: 2019] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

A **crisis of conscience **is a situation where an individual experiences an intense internal conflict between their deep-seated moral values and the actions they are pressured to take or have already taken. **Manifestation in the Public Domain**

1.** Whistleblowing against Corruption**conflict between "loyalty to the organization" and "duty to the public" triggers a crisis.

2.** Implementation of Unjust Policies-**

Eg- implementing order for eviction of slum dwellers during a harsh winter without a rehabilitation plan.

3.** Conflict between Rule of Law and Compassion- **Eg- Denying a starving,

elderly woman her rations because her biometrics failed to authenticate.

4.** Political Pressure on Neutrality- **Eg- An officer being asked to manipulate environmental impact data to favor an industrial project.

5.** Medical Ethics in Public Healthcare- **Eg- Medical triage decisions

6.** Journalists **reporting on sensitive issues like human rights violations face a clash between **"patriotism" and "truth."**

7.** Environmental Protection vs. Development- **Eg- **Aarey Forest case **in Mumbai

8.** Institutional loyalty vs public interest- **When statisticians are pressured to "under-report" inflation or unemployment figures to suit a government’s narrative.

9.** Confidentiality vs. Public Safety- **When an official knows a "state secret" that involves a threat to public health but is ordered to remain silent.

10.** Efficiency vs justice **or Speedy action vs due process. Eg- Bulldozer demolitions.

11.** Security vs rights **- Eg- Internet shutdowns in J&K

12.** Career risk vs ethical stand **- Eg- **Sanjiv Chaturvedi (IFS) **facing transfers for exposing corruption in AIIMS and HRD Ministry.

13.** Majoritarian demand vs minority rights **- Public pressure conflicting with constitutional morality. Eg-Sabrimala case

### Ethical Framework to Address Crisis of Conscience

1.** Constitutional Morality**

2.** The Public Interest Test**

3.** Gandhian Talisman**

4.** Principle of Non-Maleficence**

5.** Objectivity and Neutrality**

6.** Transparency and Accountability**

7.** Courage of Conviction**

8.** Categorical Imperative (Kant)**

9.** Integrity (Wholeness)**

10.** Seeking Guidance through "Phronesis"**

“There is a higher court than courts of justice and that is the court of conscience. It supersedes all other courts.” - **Mahatma Gandhi**

By aligning actions with the **"higher court of conscience," **a public official can ensure **human-centric, just, and truly democratic **governance.**
