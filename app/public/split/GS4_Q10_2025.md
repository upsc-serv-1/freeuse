## Question 10 (Year: 2025 | Paper: GS IV)

**Question ID: 2025-gs4-q10-civilsdaily**

**2025 - “Constitutional morality is not a natural sentiment but a product of civil education and adherence to the rule of law.” Examine the significance of constitutional morality for public servants highlighting the role in promoting good governance and ensuring accountability in public administration.** [Year: 2025] [Marks: 10]

### Answer

**Constitutional morality **implies adherence to the **core principles and spirit **of the Constitution in a democracy.

**George Grote, in his **History of Greece, **described constitutional morality as a **“rare and difficult sentiment” **involving

4. Paramount reverence for **forms and procedures **of the Constitution.

5. Adherence to law while enabling **open criticism **of authority.

6. Need for **public reason, self-restraint, and trust **in institutions.

### Significance in Promoting Good Governance

1.** Promoting Transparency **- Open processes and access to information. Eg - **The "Jan Soochna**

**Portal" in Rajasthan**

2.** Ensuring Responsiveness **- CM treats the "Right to be Heard" as a fundamental right. Eg - grievance redressal through CPGRAMS

3.** Enhancing Effectiveness **- Decisions aligned with public interest. Eg- DBT via JAM trinity

4.** Fostering Participation **- Inclusion of stakeholders in decisions. **Eg- The Nagaland "Communitisation" of Public Services**

5.** Equity and inclusiveness **- Fair distribution of resources and opportunities. Eg- **Forest Rights Act**

6.** Consensus orientation **- Mediation and dialogue over coercion. Eg- **The GST Council**

7.** Strategic vision **- Long-term constitutional goals guide policy. Eg- Sustainable development.

**8. Rule of Law - **CM ensures that the law is applied impartially, preventing "Rule by Law." **Eg-Enforcement of the Model Code of Conduct (MCC)**

### Role in ensuring accountability

1.** Legal accountability **- Eg- Strictly following the **D.K. Basu guidelines **to prevent custodial torture.

2.** Political accountability **- Responsiveness to elected representatives within limits. Eg- Parliamentary questions.

3.** Social accountability **- Eg- **MGNREGA Social Audits in Andhra Pradesh.**

4.** Financial accountability **- Prudent and transparent use of public funds. Eg- **Audit of the 2G Spectrum and Coal Block Allocations.**

5.** Professional and Administrative Accountability - **CM ensures that the "Steel Frame" maintains institutional integrity. Eg- T. N. Seshan as CEC

6.** Future accountability **- promotes "Intergenerational Equity.” Eg- EIA norms

7.** Moral Accountability - **CM prevents the "Banalization of Evil," where officers claim they were "just following orders." **Eg- Whistleblowing in the Vyapam Scam.**

**8. Procedural accountability **- Following due process and natural justice.

### Challenges

1.** Conflict with Social Morality- **Eg- sabrimala

2.** Political Populism**

3.** Legacy of Colonial Laws- **Eg- Official Secrets Act

4.** Bureaucratic Inertia- **A "Status Quo" mindset

5.** Lack of Incentives- **Eg- "Yes-men" culture

6.** Weak Civil Education**

7.** The "Neutrality vs. Commitment" Dilemma**

Constitutional morality serves as the **"North Star," **ensuring that the ship of the state remains anchored in the values of human dignity and social justice.
